How to use: Click a cell to open that library's pass-reservation page in a new tab. Click an attraction name (left column) for the museum's site. Click a town header for the library's full pass list. Hover any cell for terms (party size, restrictions).

Important: Most libraries restrict pass reservations to their own town's residents — having a Wakefield card lets you reserve at Wakefield only. If you want a different library's passes, you usually need to walk in and get a card from that town (free for any MA resident in most cases).

Library notes:
  • Wakefield — Lucius Beebe (Wakefield) NOBLE network. Free card for any MA resident with photo ID + proof of address. Open to all patrons with a Wakefield/NOBLE library card.
  • Boston — Boston Public Library Open to any MA resident. eCard issued online for digital resources only — physical card (free, in person at any branch) required for museum passes.
  • Stoneham — Stoneham Public Library NOBLE network. Free card for any MA resident with photo ID + proof of address. Non-NOBLE cards can also be added. Available to all patrons in good standing.
  • Reading — Reading Public Library NOBLE network. Free card for any MA resident. One pass per family per day; most passes once per calendar month.
  • Woburn — Woburn Public Library Museum passes restricted to Woburn residents only — even network cardholders cannot reserve. Passes restricted to Woburn residents only.
  • Lynnfield — Lynnfield Public Library Free card for any MA resident. eCard must be upgraded to full-access (in person) before reserving museum passes. Passes funded by Friends of the Lynnfield Library.
  • Peabody — Peabody Institute Library NOBLE network. Free card for any MA resident with proof of address. Available to Peabody and NOBLE network cardholders.
  • Melrose — Melrose Public Library Passes restricted to Melrose residents (limited exception for City of Melrose staff/retirees). One pass per household per day; reservations up to 30 days in advance.
  • Winchester — Winchester Public Library Passes available to Winchester residents only (with a Minuteman card). One household = 2 passes/week. Passes restricted to Winchester residents only. Specific cost details not published online; visit page for booking.
  • Saugus — Saugus Public Library NOBLE network. Any NOBLE card in good standing can reserve. ParksPass also requires MA residency. Funded by the New Friends of the Saugus Public Library.
  • Malden — Malden Public Library Anyone (MA resident) can get a Malden card, but museum-pass program is restricted to Malden residents only. Passes for Malden residents; one pass per family per 7 days.
  • Medford — Medford Public Library Museum passes generally tied to Medford residents / home library — non-residents should call to confirm. Funded by the Friends of the Medford Public Library.
  • Burlington — Burlington Public Library MVLC network. Any MVLC cardholder can reserve. Non-MVLC need Burlington work/school/property to get a card. One pass per family per day; same pass once per family per month.
  • North Reading — Flint Memorial Library (North Reading) NOBLE network (Flint Memorial). Any NOBLE cardholder can reserve passes; pickup at 1st-floor circulation. Pickup at circulation desk on the 1st floor unless otherwise noted.
  • Wilmington — Wilmington Memorial Library MVLC network. MA residents from certified towns can get a free MVLC eCard online via Wilmington — full-service, can reserve passes. Non-MVLC residents only get 30-day temp card. Passes purchased with town funds plus support from Wilmington Sons of Italy, We're One Wilmington, Community Fund, and Wilmington Arts Council. MVLC card required; cannot reserve with $20+ in fines.

Researched 2026-05-01. Pass offerings rotate seasonally — verify on the library's page before planning a trip.
