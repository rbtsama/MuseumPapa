The Salem Witch Museum
The Salem Witch Museum opened in 1972 in a historic church building and has served as a distinctive local landmark and major contributor to the city’s cultural and historic landscape since its inception.
The mission of the Salem Witch Museum is to be the voice of the innocent victims of witch hunts from 1692 to the present day. By interpreting this complicated history through an audiovisual presentation and guided tours, the museum strives to create an immersive and accessible approach to education. Virtual programming and providing educational resources allow the institution to further its mission and bring awareness to the endurance of scapegoating and injustice.
