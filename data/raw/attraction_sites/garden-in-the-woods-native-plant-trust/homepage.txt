It's Trillium Week!
Garden in the Woods
hosts
Trillium Week
, our annual celebration of this fleeting wildflower of the northern forest, through Saturday, May 9.
Come and pay homage to the many species of this tri-petaled genus in our vanted Trillium Collection. Take a drop-in Trillium Tour, and enjoy them in the spring gloaming at our beloved
Twilight Trilliums
event on Friday, May 8, 6–8 PM.
Photo: Yellow trillium (Trillium luteum), Uli Lorimer © Native Plant Trust

A strong spring fuels a year of impact—so now is the time to show your support. Make an early gift to our annual fund this spring and help power a year of abundance.
While we are off to a strong start, we need your help to keep the momentum going.
Make a gift today

Garden in the Woods Hours
Come and take in the beauty of the 2026 season at
Garden in the Woods
, our native plant botanic garden in Framingham, MA. The Garden is open daily, 10 AM–5 PM, through October 18. Our
Garden Shop

Twilight Trilliums: Friday, May 8
Twilight Trilliums is your opportunity to view our celebrated trillium collection in the beauty of dusk. This year, artist Skooby Laposky will create an immersive sound installation inspired by the natural rhythms of trilliums, and Production Horticulturist and Trillium Collection Manager Cole Campbell will share their expertise about our collection. Enjoy after-hours access to Garden in the Woods, beverages donated by Trillium Brewing and 90+ Cellars, and food from Sara’s Catering: Friday, May 8, 6-8 PM. Register
here
.
Photo: Furrowed wake-robin (Trillium sulcatum), white form, Uli Lorimer © Native Plant Trust
Growing Season Is Learning Season
With our relatively short growing season in the Northeast, spring and summer are precious to gardeners. Speed up your learning curve or keep your knowledge green with our expert-led
classes
—pollinator gardening, soils, bird-friendly gardening, designing with natives, and soil composition are among the many options we offer. A growing number of our classes are now available via Zoom, so you can learn from anywhere.
Photo: Spicebush swallowtail larvae (Papilio troilus) on host spicebush (Lindera benzoin) seedlings, Alexis Doshas © Native Plant Trust
