Admission Discounts
Memberships -
Members
visit for free! You can apply the cost of your paid admission toward the cost of your membership.
ACM -
50% discount (maximum of 6 people) by showing your membership card with a participating museum in the network. Presentation of a valid membership card is required.
ASTC Reciprocal Members -
Free admission for up to 4 people. Presentation of a valid membership card is required; the card must list one adult family member by name, and the card must have the ASTC Reciprocal Membership logo.
North American Reciprocal Museum Association
- Free admission for up to 4 people. Presentation of a valid membership card is required, and the card must have the NARM reciprocal membership sticker. One person listed on the membership card must be present at the time of visit.
Card to Culture -
$5 per person for EBT or WIC cardholders up to a total of 4 family members. A valid EBT or WIC card is required.
We are proud to participate in the
Card to Culture
program, a collaboration between
Mass Cultural Council
and the
Department of Transitional Assistance
, and
Women, Infants & Children (WIC) Nutrition Program
, by broadening accessibility to cultural programming. See the
full list
of participants in Card to Culture discounts.
Wonderfund -
$5 per person. Must show a valid Wonderfund card.
Senior Discount -
$6 off admission for each senior (age 65+).
Student Discount -
$6 off admission with valid student ID.
Active Duty Military Discount -
Free admission to the nation’s active duty military personnel including National Guard and Reserve and up to 5 of their family members each summer. A valid Military ID must be presented at check-in. This discount is available through the Blue Star Museum Program and the program begins on Armed Forces Day through Labor Day each year.
Bank of America "Museums On Us" Program -
Free admission for (only) the cardholder on the first weekend (Sat/Sun) of every month.
Group Rates -
A minimum of 10 people to book group rates. Available for school groups, community groups, clubs, and family groups; advanced reservations are required, and restrictions apply. Please call 508.929.2700 or email
reservations@ecotarium.org.

Health & Safety Guidelines
Your health and safety, and the health and safety of our staff and animals, is our priority. Please make note of the following science-based
health and safety guidelines
intended to protect you and others from getting sick.
If unwell, please stay home.
If you or anyone in your group is experiencing any
COVID-19 symptoms
, please
stay home
, and contact your health care provider.
If you or any member of your group have recently been in contact with someone confirmed or suspected of having COVID-19,
please take appropriate precautions
.
Masks are recommended but not required inside
the Museum
to reduce the risk of transmission of respiratory illness while exploring the museum’s fun interactive exhibits. Face coverings are a critical public health tool for preventing the spread of COVID-19 and protecting the health and safety of all vulnerable guests, staff, and indoor resident-animals. The EcoTarium provides
complimentary, disposable masks
at the Information Desk and Planetarium.
Continuous cleaning:
EcoTarium staff members clean the museum throughout the day.
Hand washing:
Please wash your hands and use hand sanitizer during your visit. Hand Sanitizer is available in multiple locations on each floor of the museum for your use.
Protect Wildlife:
When outdoors, please remind children to keep a 6-foot distance from our resident-wildlife, particularly the North American River Otters and Mountain Lions. These larger animals are susceptible to COVID-19. Enjoy visiting the animals, but please help us keep them healthy!
Maintain Distance when possible:
We encourage all groups to eat outside when the weather permits. Picnic tables are located at the Upper Sundial Plaza and in the Nature Explore Playground/Exhibit. There are also grassy areas near the lower parking lot that visitors may enjoy. The indoor Snack Room on the middle level has limited seating available on a first come, first served basis.
Vaccination:
Get vaccinated
against flu, COVID-19, and RSV (if eligible) to help prevent illness and severe disease.
The EcoTarium follows the
regulations and health and safety guidelines
of the Massachusetts Department of Public Health.
Visitor Code of Conduct and Group Management Policies
Your visit to the Museum should be enjoyable, educational, and fun! To ensure all visitors have a safe and memorable experience, we expect everyone to be respectful of other visitors, staff and volunteers.
Safety First:
Your health and safety, and the health and safety of our staff, volunteers and animals, is the EcoTarium’s top priority. Please follow the instructions of staff at all times. Weapons of any kind are not allowed on the property. If you see something that appears unsafe, please notify a staff member immediately.
Pets
: The EcoTarium is a wildlife zoo regulated by the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA). The USDA prohibits domestic animals anywhere on our property, including those left in vehicles. The only domestic animals allowed on the property are service animals.
Smoking:
The EcoTarium is a smoke-free facility. Smoking of any kind including vaping (the use of electronic cigarettes) is not allowed on the grounds or inside any buildings.
Alcohol:
It is the EcoTarium’s policy that ONLY alcohol provided by an EcoTarium TIPS certified staff or EcoTarium vendor may be consumed on premises. Alcoholic beverages MAY NOT be brought onto the EcoTarium’s property. City and state alcohol regulations permit the EcoTarium to offer alcohol ONLY to adults of legal drinking age and at specific licensed events. Any individual found in violation of this policy will be removed from the property.
Respect for All:
The EcoTarium values diversity and is dedicated to providing a calm and respectful experience for everyone. The EcoTarium does not tolerate discrimination or any form of harassment. The EcoTarium prohibits harassment or discrimination based on ability, age, color, ethnicity, race, family or marital status, sex, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, pregnancy and related medical conditions, language, national origin, political affiliation, religion, socioeconomic status, veteran status, health status, and other dimensions of diversity.
Cleanliness:
Please help us maintain a clean facility by picking up your trash and placing it in the trash bins.
Group Management:
We want all children to have a successful visit! If you are chaperoning children who have been on a school bus for a long ride, we recommend planning your visit to begin outside in the playground area to allow children to release some of their energy before coming into the museum. Our exhibits are expensive and in some cases irreplaceable and should be treated with care. Graffiti can cause permanent damage to an exhibit. Please remind all children to treat the museum’s objects and animals with respect.
Fun:
Exhibits and programs at the EcoTarium change regularly. You may notice some exhibits and experiences have returned for summer, such as the popular “Bubbles” exhibit, and others may be arriving soon. For regular updates on exhibits please visit the
indoor exploration
and
outdoor exploration
pages of our website.
Museum Amenities
Food and Drink
The EcoTarium hosts a prepared food café operated by
Garden Fresh
, open 11:00 am to 3:00 pm, that offers sandwiches, beverages, snacks, and ice cream treats! We also have a variety of snack and drink vending machines currently located in the Hirsh Center on the lower level or in our gift shop on the upper level of the museum. In the warmer months, you can find our food offering outside at the Trailhead Café, and during the colder months operating on our lower level outside the Hirsh Center.
Our scenic grounds provide plenty of perfect picnicking spots and indoor eating
spots
inside the Hirsh Center. In the warmer weather, sit under the umbrellas at the Trailhead Café, tables located on Sundial Plaza, WildCat Station, Nature Explore, or spread out at a picnic table under a shady tree.
The EcoTarium does not serve dinner onsite. Many dining options are available nearby, ranging from casual to full-service restaurants. The Guest Experience team can provide recommendations. Same-day reentry is permitted with your original admission receipt.
State law prohibits guests from bringing alcoholic beverages onto the museum grounds.
Parking
Parking is always free and the EcoTarium is conveniently located just minutes from major highway routes, including I-290 and I-90 (Mass Pike).
Shop at the Museum
EcoTarium's Museum Shop shelves are overflowing with unique treasures and fun gifts to fit all ages, interests and budgets. Come browse our wide selection of plush toys and puppets, science kits, children's books, giftware, field guides, and plenty more.
EcoTarium members receive 10% off purchases in the Museum Shop! Located on the Upper Level of the museum, admission is waived for those who only have time to shop.
Your purchases support our mission, innovative programs, exhibits and wildlife.
