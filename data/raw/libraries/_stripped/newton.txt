Library Cards & Borrowing

Get A Library Card

Welcome to Newton Free Library! Our collection of over 500,000 books, CDS, DVDS, magazines, and online resources is yours to borrow or stream! We invite you to sign up for a library card so you can take advantage of all the Library has to offer.

Requirements: Proof of Address & Photo ID

Apply for a library card in person at the Newton Free Library or at any other Minuteman library with a photo ID and proof of address .

Proof of address can be a drivers license, a lease agreement, or something that was mailed to you (Amazon, a bill, etc) .

Personal letters, postmarked within 60 days, can be used as proof of current local address.

For patrons who cannot produce photo identification or proof of current local address, someone in an official capacity may attest to a person’s identification and/or address (e.g., someone who works in an official capacity at a shelter to attest to the identification and/or address of a person).

MA Residents (who do not live in Newton)

Anyone with a current address in Massachusetts is eligible to get a Newton Free Library card.

While anyone in Massachusetts can get a Newton library card, some resources are only available for Newton Residents including, but not limited to: hoopla, kanopy, and naxos. For specific information about access to another database or digital resource if you are not a Newton Resident, please contact the library for more information.

Get an eCard now!

If you are looking for access to electronic resources while the Library is not open, you can sign up for an eCard and get started right away. eCards do not include access to our physical collection and museum passes. Visit Newton Free Library in person with your ID and proof of address to receive your library card with full borrowing privileges, including our physical collection and museum passes.

Sign up for an online card

Library Cards for Children

Children 5 years or older, or in kindergarten, are eligible for their own library card.

Children under the age of 12 may apply for a library card if they are accompanied by a parent or legal guardian who has their photo ID and proof of address to use as confirmation. The child must be present for a parent or legal guardian to get them a library card.

Library Cards for Tweens and Teens

Tweens and teens 12 to 17 may present a school ID in place of a government issued ID to apply for a library card, or log into their school portal to confirm identity.

If the applicant is 17 years old or younger and cannot meet the ID requirement, a parent or guardian must provide proof of identification and address for them. The tween/teen must be present for a parent or legal guardian to get them a library card.

Library Cards for Out-of-state Residents

Out-of-State Residents, who meet the following requirements, may be issued a library card with sufficient identification:

Work in Massachusetts

Attend school in Massachusetts

Own property in Massachusetts

Are temporarily living in Massachusetts for more than 2 months

Out of state residents must provide identification showing their home/permanent address.

Property owners must provide verification such as tax bill, utility bill, etc for their Massachusetts property.

Students must verify their status with school identification and provide their school address.

Out-of-state residents working in Massachusetts must provide their work address.

Patrons who are temporary residents (such as college students or au pairs) should also bring proof of permanent address.

Important: While out-of-state residents can get a Newton library card, some resources are only available for Newton Residents including, but not limited to: hoopla, kanopy, and naxos. For specific information about access to another database or digital resource if you are not a Newton Resident, please contact the library for more information.

Renew Your Library Card

Click here to Renew Your Library Card Online . When logged into your Library Account in the library catalog, you can also access this link via the blue “Renew your card” button that will appear only when your card has expired or is within 30 days of expiration. If you are unable to renew online, please see the information below on how to renew your card with us.

You can also renew your card either over email at newtonpublicservices@minlib.net , by calling us at (617) 796-1360, coming in person to do so anytime we are open.

To renew your account over email, please confirm your current contact information so we can make sure our records are up to date:

Your mailing address

Your phone number

Your e-mail address

Your library card number (no spaces)

Once these details are confirmed, we can update your Minuteman Library account for another 5 years.

Using Your Card and Borrowing Policies

The Newton Free Library is a member of the Minuteman Library Network . Your library card entitles you to borrow materials from the Newton Free Library as well as 43 other public and academic libraries in the Metrowest region. Borrowing policies vary depending on which Minuteman Library you visit, but all items checked out in Newton are loaned under our borrowing policy.

Privacy

Newton Free Library is dedicated to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of our borrowers. We take every measure to protect your personal information and checkout history.

If you send someone to pick up your hold, they will need a copy of your card or a copy of the hold pickup notification email to checkout the item(s).

Managing Your Library Account Online

You can keep track of your library account online using Minuteman Library Network’s “My Account”, which allows you to place holds, renew eligible items, keep track of requests and checked out items, and more.

Access My Account

To set up your account, you will need your library card.

Go to the My Account login page .

If this is your first time logging on and you do not have a password set up first, click on Reset or Create New Password and follow the instructions there.

To login to your account, visit our homepage or the catalog and click “Sign In” in the upper right hand corner.

*You will also use this password, (or PIN – Personal Identification Number), to take advantage of the Library’s digital and streaming services via Libby.

Forgot your password/PIN? You can reset it from the “My Account” log in screen at any time.

Minuteman Library Network App

Minuteman Library Network App

Access your library card from your smartphone using the Minuteman Library Network app, available from the Apple Store or Google Play Store .

From the app you’ll be able to:

Store your library card barcode for checkout at circulation desks and self check out machines

Renew items

Place, suspend, and cancel holds

Find and request items

Find the Library’s event calendar and museum passes

Contact the Library

Access the Library’s digital collections and resources

More FAQs about the Minuteman Library App are available at the very bottom of the Network’s FAQ page .

Holds/Requests

To reserve an item you need your library card number and password. You can place a hold online or in-person or you can call the Library at 617-796-1360.

You may not place a hold on:

Reference materials

Speed Read books

Laptops and Hotspots

You will receive an email or phone call when your hold is ready for pick-up. Holds must be picked up within seven days.

Holds must be checked out on the library card of the borrower who has placed the hold. This policy is in accordance with Massachusetts General Law, Chapter 78, Section 7, 1994. Reserves cannot be released to any individual other than the cardholder unless the individual has the library card or hold pickup notification email.

Returns

Items can be returned:

through our automated Book Return sorter, located inside the Library near the parking lot entrance;

to the exterior book drop, located outside the Homer Street entrance for 24/7 returns;

to any Minuteman Library.

Please note some items (Commonwealth Catalog items, Inter-Library Loan items, Library of Things items, Museum/Recreation Passes) must be returned directly to the Newton Free Library. These items are labeled accordingly.

Lost or Damaged Items

Items that are lost or returned in condition unsuitable for future use will be billed to the patron at the price listed in the catalog.

We do not accept replacement copies.

We are unable to issue a refund after payment has been made.

Institutional Cards

About Institutional Library Cards

The Newton Free Library is pleased to offer a special card to assist the needs of businesses, nursing homes, and other institutions in Newton. Institutional Library Cards are for organizations that require several authorized individuals to have access to a single account. These Library Cards are not meant for personal use. Please note that schools are not eligible for Institutional Library Cards.

Institutional Library Card Application

Application Requirements

After submitting an application, send a letter on your institution’s letterhead to newtonpublicservices@minlib.net or to: Newton Free Library Borrower Services 330 Homer Street Newton, MA 02459

Letter should state which staff are authorized to use this card.

Letter should be signed by appropriate authority at institution.

Letter should agree to accept responsibility for all items borrowed on the account.

Use of Institutional Cards

The institution is responsible for overdue, damaged, lost, or stolen materials.

The card is accepted only at the Newton Free Library, not at other Minuteman Libraries.

Checkouts on this card will be for:6 weeks

No renewals

No requests

Institutional Cards may not be used for eResources.
