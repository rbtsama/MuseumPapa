Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander Heritage Month
This May we’re thanking the local Bengali community’s contribution to the exhibition “Divine Color: Hindu Prints from Modern Bengal.”
In the “Prints in Use: Devotion, Storytelling, Decoration” gallery, three sound stations present recordings read by members of the local Bengali community . The gallery explores how Bengali prints are used in homes, where people engage with images of Hindu gods in many ways, most prominently through intimate expressions of devotion.
These audio experiences are paired with colorful artworks to explore how love, care, and devotion to the gods come alive through storytelling, poetry, and music. Discover the immersive audio recordings for yourself in Torf Gallery through May 31.

Jewish American Heritage Month
Havdalah spice boxes are features of the end of Shabbat—the Jewish day of rest—on Saturday afternoon or evening. Participants smell the spices during the Havdalah ritual, which aims to comfort and revive the spirit before the beginning of the new week.
These vessels can look like towers or boxes, or take on a more modern, whimsical style like artist Deborah Krupenia’s . This shallow container is made of shakudo, a combination of copper and gold. The convex oval body is composed of two parts soldered together, while a circular lid with a cone-like finial sits in a small depression at center.
The decoration consists of a field of radiating gold dots that become larger and lighter in color as they move toward the edge. The bigger dots have holes in their centers to allow the scent of spices to emerge from the vessel. See Krupenia’s work on view in “Intentional Beauty: Jewish Ritual Art from the Collection.”

Boston Family Days

Credit

Photo courtesy of the Boston Mayor’s Office—Jeremiah Robinson.

Credit

Photo courtesy of the Boston Mayor’s Office—Jeremiah Robinson.

Thanks to a partnership between the MFA and the City of Boston, Boston Family Days provide students who live in Boston and their families free access to the Museum on the first and second Sundays of each month. Learn more about how to participate, including how to register for the program.
The MFA offers a variety of special gallery activities on Boston Family Days. Check below for the full schedules by date. We look forward to welcoming you to the Museum!

Programs
Gallery Explorations
Discover art in different areas of the Museum with 30-minute themed tours and activities for all ages.

Drop-In Art Making
Art supplies are provided for these free art-making activities inspired by works in the collection.

Discovery Cart
Explore the materials and techniques artists use through discussion and demonstration.

Teen Sundays
Join us for a variety of activities geared toward teens, like tours, art making, and more.

UNIQLO Story Hour
Drop by for an interactive children’s story program.

Discovery Guides

Explore the MFA with in-gallery guides that are fun for all ages! Search for imagery, learn about the art, and get creative with drawing and writing prompts. Each activity focuses on a specific theme, linking objects across many time periods and cultures. Pick them up at Sharf Visitor Center on your next visit!

Ticket Information
Last entry 30 minutes before Museum closing.
Tickets are available for purchase in person (pending exhibition ticket availability) or in advance on mfa.org or by phone at 1-800-440-6975. $6 per-order processing fee applies to all phone orders.
General admission tickets are valid for the Museum’s entire operating hours on the given visit day. General admission tickets do not include access to “Framing Nature: Gardens and Imagination.”

Access to “Framing Nature: Gardens and Imagination” requires a specific exhibition ticket with a timed-entry time slot for the gallery. Entry time slots are 30 minutes; visitors must enter the exhibition within that 30-minute window.
Once inside the exhibition, visitors may stay as long as they like.
No re-entry into a special exhibition.
If you’re unable to secure tickets in your cart for your entire party within one time slot, please choose a different time that can accommodate your entire group.
Make sure to add any free youth tickets to your order at the same time as you add adult tickets.

For more information about purchasing, using, and exchanging tickets, please see Ticketing Policies .
Member Tickets
Members are required to reserve a timed-entry ticket for special exhibitions and are encouraged to reserve exhibition tickets in advance as time slots may sell out. See Membership FAQ for detailed information.

See Free and Discounted Admission for all the opportunities to visit the Museum for free or with discounted admission tickets.

A College Student’s Guide to the MFA

Keeping the MFA a Safe and Welcoming Space For All

The MFA is committed to being a place where everyone belongs. This is only truly possible when our visitors, volunteers, and staff recognize and honor one another with dignity and respect. We have no tolerance for any acts of harassment or harmful behavior. Your words and actions matter. Thank you for keeping the MFA a safe and welcoming space for all.

A Great Place to Start
Only have an hour or not sure where to start? Take a self-guided highlights tour that spans the MFA’s global collection from ancient times to today. MFA Mobile on Bloomberg Connects offers rich multimedia content to amplify your in-gallery experience or explore from home.
Explore MFA Mobile

Have Questions? Start Here
Tickets
For more information and to reserve tickets, visit Tickets .
Do I need a ticket for my child or baby even though they are admitted for free?
Yes, all visitors regardless of age are required to have a ticket.

Can I purchase tickets in advance?
Yes, we recommend purchasing tickets in advance to ensure availability and ease of entry into the Museum.

How do I present tickets purchased or reserved online at the door for entry?
Visitors and members who reserve tickets in advance are encouraged to select the “Print at Home” option to receive tickets electronically. Tickets may be presented on a mobile device or printed on paper. All tickets will be scanned at the door. To speed up your entry, have your tickets ready when you arrive at the Museum.

Can I return or exchange tickets if my plans change?
See our Ticketing Policies for more information and how to contact us.

Can I reserve tickets for a group?
Guided and self-guided visits are available for PreK–12 audiences, Access programs, and college groups that are part of the University Members program . Guided tours are not currently available for other adult groups.
To reserve your group visit, see Groups .
Bulk Ticket Discount
If you’re visiting with 15 or more nonmember adults who don’t fall into one of the above audiences, you can receive a discount on your bulk ticket order. See Tickets for more information.

Preparing for Your Visit
Is the Fenway Entrance open?
No, the Fenway Entrance is currently closed.

Is there a coat check?
Yes, we offer complimentary coat check at the Huntington Avenue Entrance.
All bags are subject to inspection. Bags smaller than 11 x 15 inches are welcome. Backpacks must be carried as a handbag or worn on the front side of the body. Bags in excess of 11 x 15 inches containing needed medical equipment, or for use in nursing or baby care, are allowed in the Museum if tagged at arrival.
Bag restrictions for Museum events may vary. Please refer to the specific event page for details.

Can I visit a gift shop without buying a ticket?
The Signature Shop, located at the Huntington Avenue Entrance, can be visited without a ticket.

Can I get something to eat?
Please visit Dining for more information about the Museum's dining venues.

Are all the galleries open?
See Gallery Updates for information about closures. Please note, galleries may be closed temporarily without notice.

Can we stay in the Museum as long as we like?
Your ticket is valid until the Museum closes, but there is no re-entry to special exhibitions.

Do you offer guided tours?
Please see Galley Tours and Talks for the full schedule of guided tours.

Members
For more member information, visit Membership FAQ .
Can I use my membership card for admission?
For general admission, membership cards admit MFA members daily per the limits of their membership level. For special exhibitions, tickets are required for all visitors (members, nonmembers, children); members cannot gain entry to special exhibitions with their membership card alone. Members have access to free or discounted special exhibition tickets, limited to the benefits of their membership level. We encourage all members to reserve tickets in advance online or by phone.

If my plans change, can I exchange or return my special exhibition ticket?
Yes. We know sometimes plans change—if yours do, please help more people in our community enjoy the Museum by rebooking, exchanging, or canceling your tickets ahead of time. We hope you come to the Museum as planned, but if you decide not to, other visitors might miss out if you don’t use your timed-entry tickets.
Members, if you need to reschedule:
To reschedule special exhibition or any paid tickets, contact us in advance of your visit date by calling 1-800-440-6975 to exchange your tickets for another date. Tickets are non-refundable, but can be exchanged prior to the day of your visit. A $4 exchange fee applies.
If you do not arrive for a special exhibition, you will forfeit your limited free exhibition ticket(s) and we will not be able to reschedule you for another time slot.

How do university or corporate members reserve tickets?
For details on how to access free or discounted university or corporate member tickets, please visit the Special Offers section on the Tickets page.

How do I join or renew my membership?
You can join or renew onsite, over the phone, online , or send a check by mail to:
Membership Museum of Fine Arts, Boston 465 Huntington Avenue Boston, MA 02115

Accessibility
The Museum is committed to accessibility for all visitors. For general access information, please see Accessibility , call 617-267-9300, or e-mail [email protected] .
Are wheelchairs available?
Yes, wheelchairs are available to borrow on a first-come, first-served basis. See Accessibility for more information.

Are gallery stools available?
Yes, gallery stools are available to borrow on a first-come, first-served basis from the Sharf Visitor Center.

Is the Museum wheelchair accessible?
Yes, the Museum is fully wheelchair accessible.

May I bring my service animal?
The MFA warmly welcomes service dogs. Please note, emotional support animals and comfort animals are not permitted.

What if I need a special accommodation?
Please visit Accessibility to see if your request is addressed. If not please contact 617-267-9300 or e-mail [email protected] for more information.
