Directions

The Aviation Museum’s grounds are excellent for plane-watching and family picnics, too!

The Aviation Museum of N.H. is located at 27 Navigator Road, Londonderry, N.H.

We’re at Manchester-Boston Regional Airport, but we’re on the other side of the field opposite the passenger terminal and control tower.

From the north:

• Take I-293 to Exit 2, South Willow Street. (Near the Mall of N.H.) Go south on South Willow Street, watching for brown “Aviation Museum” signs. Make a right onto Harvey Road. Make a right onto Navigator Road. Then follow Navigator Road to bottom of the hill. Aviation Museum is on the left.

From the east:

• Take Route 101 to I-293 in Manchester. Take Exit 2, South Willow Street. (Near the Mall of N.H.) Go south on South Willow Street, watching for brown “Aviation Museum” signs. Make a right onto Harvey Road. Make a right onto Navigator Road. Then follow Navigator Road to bottom of the hill. Aviation Museum is on the left.

From the west:

• Take Route 101 to I-293 in Manchester. Take Exit 2, South Willow Street. (Near the Mall of N.H.) Go south on South Willow Street, watching for brown “Aviation Museum” signs. Make a right onto Harvey Road. Make a right onto Navigator Road. Then follow Navigator Road to bottom of the hill. Aviation Museum is on the left.

From the south via I-93

• Take I-93 to North Londonderry, Exit 5. At bottom of ramp, make left onto Route 28. Follow Route 28, looking for signalled intersection with Page Street. Make left onto Page Street, which turns into Grenier Field Road. At next light, continue straight; road is now Harvey Road. At top of hill, look for intersection with Navigator Road on left. Make left onto Navigator Road. Then follow Navigator Road to bottom of the hill. Aviation Museum is on the left.

From the south via F.E. Everett Turnpike

• Take F.E. Everett Turnpike north to Exit 13/Manchester Airport. Take Exit 13 and cross bridge over Merrimack River At first traffic light, make right onto Pettingill Road. Follow Pettingill Road straight through three traffic lights. After third light, road is renamed Harvey Road. Follow Harvey Road as it bears to right, then at traffic light make a left. Follow Harvey Road up short hill. At top of hill, look for intersection with Navigator Road on left. Make left onto Navigator Road. Then follow Navigator Road to bottom of the hill. Aviation Museum is on the left.

Hours:

The Aviation Museum of N.H. is open to the public:

• Wednesdays through Saturdays 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.

• Sundays 1 to 4 p.m.

• Other times by appointment. Call (603) 669-4820 ext. 404 to arrange for a group tour or private visit.

Special 2025 holiday season hours:

• The Aviation Museum will be closed on Wednesday, Nov. 26 and Thursday, Nov. 27 for Thanksgiving.

• The Aviation Museum STORE ONLY will be open extra days for holiday shopping from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. on Monday, Dec. 15 ; Tuesday, Dec. 16 ; Monday, Dec. 22; and Tuesday, Dec. 23.

• The Aviation Museum will be closed on Wednesday, Dec. 24 and Thursday, Dec. 25 for Christmas.

• The Aviation museum will be OPEN EXTRA DAYS on Monday, Dec. 29 and Tuesday, Dec. 30 for holiday visits.

• The Aviation Museum will be closed on Thursday, Jan. 1, 2026 for New Year’s Day.

Admission prices:

• Adults 13 and above, $10.

• Children age 6 to 12, $5.

• Kids 5 and under, FREE!

• Seniors (65+), veterans, active military, $5.

• Aviation Museum members, FREE!

• Library Pass: Maximum of four people on one pass; additional members in party must pay appropriate admission fee.

The Museum is staffed by volunteers of the New Hampshire Aviation Historical Society and all admission fees and donations support exhibits, education programs, operations and maintenance.

Visitors! Take control of a vintage B-24 bomber with our ‘D-Day Flight Adventure’ June 5-7

The virtual B-24 Liberator, nicknamed “Hellzadroppin”, that Aviation Museum visitors can pilot over France’s Normandy coast on the Elite Simulator.

Mark this year’s D-Day anniversary (coming up on June 6) by taking the controls of a B-24 Liberator flying over the beaches in Normandy, and then landing at the key airfield of Caen, France! This exciting ‘D-Day Flight Adventure’ will be available to visitors for a limited time this week at the Aviation Museum of New Hampshire ‘s Elite Flight Simulator. If you’ve ever wondered what it’s like to pilot this iconic aircraft that helped the Allies win World War II, here’s your chance!

Gary Johnson and Steve Heffelfinger, volunteers at the Aviation Museum of N.H., confer while planning the museum’s ‘D-Day Flight Adventure’ simulation of a B-24 Liberator flying over France’s Normandy coast.

From Thursday, June 5 through Saturday, June 7, visitors age 13 and above can pilot a virtual B-24, one of the many types of aircraft that took part in the Allied D-Day invasion of France. The pivotal operation began on June 6, 1944 and hastened the victory over Germany in World War II.

Each B-24 flight, including an attempt to land at the Caen airfield, will last about 10 minutes. As you roar over iconic sights such as Utah and Omaha beaches, you’ll be guided by knowledgeable Aviation Museum volunteers. They’ll combine flight instruction with historic context about what remains the largest amphibious military operation in world history—one with ties to Grenier Air Base back in the U.S., now today’s Manchester-Boston Regional Airport .

The Aviation Museum’s B-24 ‘D-Day Flight Adventure’ simulation will operate Thursday, June 5 through Saturday, June 7 from 1 to 4 p.m. each day and is included with museum admission. No reservations required—first come, first served. Join us this week as we honor the 81st anniversary of this key turning point in World War II!

A view from behind the B-24 on final approach to the airfield outside Caen, France.
