Admission Fees

Buy Tickets

Tickets are also available at the door.

$10.00 Adults (aged 18-64) — $5.00 Children and Teens (aged 6-17) College Students (with current ID) Seniors (65 and over) Active Duty Military Personnel — FREE Admission Museum Members & children aged 5 and under

HOURS of OPERATION:
Wednesday - Friday: 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM Saturday: 10:00 AM - 2:30 PM final admission is 30 minutes before closing
Hours subject to change due to special and private events.
Closed Thanksgiving, Christmas and New Year’s Day.

Discounts:

NARM : North American Reciprocal Museum Association.
We are excited to share with you that we recently joined “NARM,” the North American Reciprocal Museum Association. This now entitles members of the Charles River Museum at the Supporter Level ($150) or higher to all the benefits of reciprocal memberships at dozens of museums in our region and over 1,300 museums across the country.

Massachusetts K-12, College and University Educators
Massachusetts education professionals receive a discounted admission rate of $5.00 when presenting a valid work ID or proof of affiliation.
AAA
Visitors get 20% off General Museum Admission upon presentation of a valid AAA card.
EBT
EBT cardholders receive 50% off regular admission.
We are proud to participate in Mass Cultural Council's Card to Culture program in collaboration with the Department of Transitional Assistance, the Department of Public Health's WIC Nutrition Program, the Massachusetts Health Connector, and hundreds of organizations by making cultural programming accessible to those for whom cost is a participation barrier. See the complete list of participating organizations offering EBT , WIC , Wonderfund , and ConnectorCare discounts.
SUN Bucks
Mass Cultural Council's Card to Culture program ’s Sun Bucks accepted from June 17 - September 1, 2025.

Parking Information & Directions
IMPORTANT PARKING NOTICE:
There is NO Museum Visitor Parking in the Francis Cabot Lowell Mill lots where we are located.
ALL vehicles without a Mill-approved Parking Permit, including handicap permitted vehicles, WILL BE SUBJECT TO TOW by Mill Management This applies to ALL Museum Visitors, including Private Events. Please seek parking on street or at other public lots. For those with Accessibility considerations, please visit HERE.

The video above will give you fun and easy advice on locating our Visitor’s Entrance!

Closest Lots (by distance)
Embassy Public Parking Garage, 42 Cooper St Carter Street Lot, 3 Carter St Crescent Street Parking Lot, 24 Crescent St Walnut Street Lot, 41 Walnut St Common Street Lot, 68 Common St.
Parking is $1.00/hour in all municipal parking lots. A full day to park is $6.00 . ( see map of lots )
From there, it is a short walk to our Visitor Entrance doors.

REMINDER: the Museum has NO On-Site Parking of our own.
Parking in the Francis Cabot Lowell Mill Lots will result in TOWING by the Mill Management

Click to map below to open full size

View fullsize

Once you've parked in the Cooper Street lot or the adjacent Embassy Parking Garage, all you need do is take a short but thoroughly enjoyable, scenic walk on the Landry footbridge across the Charles River, turning to your right (to the East) after you've crossed, and you'll see the Museum entrance about 100 yards ahead. Enjoy your walk along the Charles! - Please note that the footbridge is clearly indicated on the map below, immediately above (north) of the Embassy Parking Facility. CLICK HERE TO SEE A GOOGLE MAP of the EMBASSY PARKING FACILITY﻿

CLICK HERE for the City of Waltham’s page on Parking in Waltham

Additional area public parking is readily available, with the closest lots being along Moody Street
You can see our proximity to the Waltham Center MBTA Fitchburg Line Commuter Rail stop, as well as a number of MBTA bus lines, including the 70, 505, 553, 554, 556, and 558 routes.

Enhance your visit! We're now on Bloomberg Connects!

May 27, 2026 /

Joe Niedbala

"Bloomberg Connects is a free digital guide that puts thousands of cultural institutions right in your pocket. Whether you are exploring locally or traveling, it offers expert-curated audio, video, and text content for more than 1,250 museums, sculpture parks, botanical gardens, and galleries globally." ﻿ ﻿﻿ ﻿We are proud to now be part of this wonderful, FREE platform that allows app users a taste of the Charles River Museum of Industry & Innovation experience. And Museum Visitors using the app have access to an enhanced digital experience, including audio, video, and expanded content beyond what we have on site.
When you visit us next, look for QR codes placed throughout the Museum that link to the Bloomberg Connects app and our content. These are placed where we have provided enhanced content, both audio and video, to add to your enjoyment and learning.
And we'll be expanding these experiences over time to always improve your Visitor experience and make us a better Charles River Museum!
