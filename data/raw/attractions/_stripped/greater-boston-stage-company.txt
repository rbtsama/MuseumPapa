ANNIE

Leapin’ lizards! Everyone’s favorite family musical comes to GBSC for the first time! Annie is the heartwarming, Tony Award-winning classic that follows the adventures of a spirited young orphan determined to find her parents. Set in 1930s New York City, Annie’s journey takes her from the strict orphanage run by the cruel Miss Hannigan to the lavish home of billionaire Oliver Warbucks. With the help of her dog Sandy and a little “Tomorrow” optimism, Annie discovers the true meaning of family in this beloved musical filled with unforgettable songs and big-hearted charm.

BROADWAY BOOTCAMP

The Young Company’s Broadway Bootcamp is a high-energy, 5-day intensive designed to help students strengthen their audition skills and build confidence through personalized coaching and masterclasses led by theatre professionals and guest artists. The week concludes with a special evening at Greater Boston Stage Company’s Annie , including a panel discussion with cast members. Two sessions are available: Session One for Acts 1 & 2 (Grades 4–7) and Session Two for Acts 2 & 3 (Grades 7–12). Perfect for students ready to elevate their audition technique and shine on stage!

WAYS TO GIVE

Help Us End Season 26 Strong!

Greater Boston Stage Company is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to bringing professional live theatre and theatre education to the heart of the Stoneham community. Because ticket sales cover only about half of our annual operating budget, your tax-deductible donation plays a vital role in sustaining our work and strengthening the local economy. Whether you choose to make a one-time gift, become a recurring donor, contribute through your business, or offer in-kind support, there are many ways to make an impact. Help keep exceptional theatre and arts education thriving at GBSC for seasons to come.

Directions to Greater Boston Stage Company

FROM NEW HAMPSHIRE AND POINTS NORTH Follow I-93 South to Exit 27. Take a left at the light at the end of the ramp. Follow Montvale Avenue to Main Street and take a right. We’re 1/2 mile ahead on the right.

FROM THE NORTH SHORE Follow I-95/128 South to Exit 56A. Continue onto Route 28 South. We’re 2 miles ahead on the right.

FROM BOSTON AND POINTS SOUTH Follow I-93 North to Exit 25. Continue onto Route 28 North. We’re 1 mile ahead on the left.

FROM THE SOUTH SHORE Follow I-95/128 North to Exit 56A. Continue onto Route 28 South. We’re 2 miles ahead on the right.

BY BUS

As of March 19, 2016, the MBTA Route 132 bus extended its hours of operation, and Stoneham has a new express bus service. For more information, visit http://www.mbta.com/schedules_and_maps/bus/routes/?route=132 .

Route 132 – Weekday service on the 132 will now run from 6:00am to 11:30pm from Redstone to Oak Grove and Malden Station, and from 5:30am to 11pm from Malden Station to Redstone. The additional departure times from Redstone Shopping Center to Oak Grove and Malden Center will be 7:30pm, 8:30pm, 9:30pm, 10:30pm and 11:30pm. The additional departure times from Malden Center to Redstone will be 8:00pm, 9:00pm, 10:00pm and 11:00pm. Please note that the last bus will leave Malden Center for Redstone at 11pm, and the last bus from Redstone to Oak Grove and Malden Center will leave Redstone at 11:30pm.

Route 325 – Also starting on March 19, there will be one express trip that leaves Redstone Shopping Center at 7:05am for Haymarket, and the return trip will leave Haymarket at 5:20PM for Redstone Shopping Center. The Stoneham Transportation Advisory Committee (STAC) would like to thank State Representative Michael Day and MBTA officials for working closely together to make this happen.

Group Transportation

Traveling with a group to the show? Consider renting a charter bus through our partners at CharterUP . Boston charter buses are also available through GOGO Charters and Bus.com , both offering group transportation that you can book online. For groups of all sizes, local bus rentals are also offered through Boston Charter Bus Rentals and Boston Charter Bus Services .

Pricing

Box Office

Hours: noon to 4pm, Tuesday through Friday Phone number: 781-279-2200 Email: boxoffice@greaterbostonstage.org

All sales are final and non-refundable. Ticket sales are not tax deductible. Discounts or special offers must be mentioned at time of purchase, and multiple discounts cannot be combined; proper ID and/or verification of discounts are required to redeem tickets. No retroactive discounts will be given. Schedules are subject to change. Tickets may be exchanged up to 24 hours prior to the performance for a different performance of the same production per order. Non-member orders are assessed a $5.00 per ticket handling fee for phone orders and $5.00 per ticket online, which enables Greater Boston Stage Company to keep overall prices low and production values high. Fees are waived for walk-up customers at the Box Office. Subscribers are always offered the best seats available.

Special Pricing

26 FOR $26

To ensure everyone has the opportunity to enjoy live theatre, we are offering 26 tickets at just $26 each for every mainstage production. These special tickets are available on a first-come, first-served basis and are intended for those who may not be able to afford regular-priced tickets. Reserve yours by calling our box office at 781-279-2200. Please note, there is a limit of 2 tickets per patron.

FRUGAL FRIDAYS

Frugal Friday tickets are now available online! Use code FRUGAL (set to go live at noon on Frugal Friday) to purchase $26 tickets for the first performance of each Mainstage show. Tickets can also be reserved in person or by calling (781) 279-2200 starting at noon on the day of the performance. Limit 4 tickets per patron. Join us in making live theatre accessible to all!

SAVVY SATURDAYS

Savvy Saturday tickets are now available online! Use code SAVVY to purchase $45 tickets for the 7 PM Saturday performances of each Mainstage show. These discounted tickets are limited, so get them while they last! Limit 4 tickets per customer. Tickets can also be reserved by calling (781) 279-2200 or visiting the box office.

CARD TO CULTURE

Massachusetts residents who present Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) cards at the Box Office may receive up to six $6 tickets per card for Greater Boston Stage Company Mainstage and Young Company productions. This discount does not apply to Special Events. This includes SNAP, WIC and similar programs.

MASSACHUSETTS TEACHERS ASSOCIATION DISCOUNT

MTA members receive a $5 discount on regular price tickets to any mainstage production. Additionally, all fees will be waived. Based on availability. Discounts must be mentioned at the time of purchase. Use code MTA5 .

MILITARY DISCOUNT

All active duty, veteran or retired military personnel receive $5 off all Greater Boston Stage Company Mainstage ticket purchases. Discount must be mentioned at time of purchase. An ID card or proof of service may be required.

SENIOR DISCOUNT

All patrons age 62+ are eligible to receive $5 off select Greater Boston Stage Company mainstage and concert/event ticket purchases. Discount must be mentioned at time of purchase.

STUDENT TICKETS

Mainstage tickets for all children and students (including college and graduate school) are available for $26. Use code STUDENT when purchasing tickets or contact the box office at 781-279-2200.

GROUP DISCOUNTS

Groups of 10+ qualify for the group rate. Contact the box office at 781-279-2200 or boxoffice @greaterbostonstage.org for more information.

GENERAL POLICIES

Smoking is permitted outdoors only.

Babes in arms are not permitted in the theatre.

Outside food and drink are not permitted in the theatre.

Please turn off all pages, cellular phones, watch alarms and other noisemaking equipment when in the theatre.

The photographing or sound recording of any performance or the possession of any device for such photographing or sound recording in this theatre is prohibited by law. Violations may render the offender liable to financial charges. Violators may be punished by confiscation of equipment and ejection.

Greater Boston Stage Company reserves the right to ask any patron causing a disturbance to leave the premises without refund.

Greater Boston Stage Company rarely cancels performances due to bad weather. If you absolutely cannot make it to the theatre, please call the Box Office before your scheduled performance. The Box Office will make all attempts to reschedule you for a different performance of the same show. No refunds will be issued.

LATECOMERS All latecomers will be seated at the discretion of the management in order to minimize distractions in our intimate theatre space. Latecomers may be required to sit in the rear of the theatre. Refunds/exchanges will not be issued to latecomers.

AGE APPROPRIATENESS We rarely suggest appropriate ages for our productions. We assume all children are different and that parents and guardians are the best judges for what’s appropriate. We are happy to provide as much information as possible to help make these decisions. If you have questions, please call.

Theatre for Young Audiences

We are THRILLED to announce the continuation of our Theatre for Young Audiences Series!

TheaterWorks USA, the country’s preeminent touring theatre company for young audiences, returns to Greater Boston Stage Company for our 26th season! Each fun production will offer weekday student matinees and a public performance. In addition, GBSC is thrilled to include Annie: The Broadway Musical as part of the series this season!

Student Matinee Tickets for School Groups: $22

1 complimentary teacher/chaperone ticket included for every 15 students. All fees waived!

Contact Bryan Miner for field trip/student matinee bookings! bryan@greaterbostonstage.org (781) 587-7907

CLICK, CLACK, MOO April 2026

Student Matinees: April 16 and 17 at 9:30am and 11:45 am.
Public Performance: April 17 at 7pm.
“Cows that type? Hens on strike! Whoever heard of such a thing!” Farmer Brown cries.
When his granddaughter Jenny comes for a visit, Farmer Brown declares the farm a tech-free zone. He takes her laptop in the cold barn with the shivering cows who use her computer to type messages requesting blankets.
“No way,” replies Farmer Brown. “No blankets!”
So the cows go on strike and the chickens join them in solidarity. No blankets? No milk! No eggs!
Will Farmer Brown give in to the animals’ demands? Will Jenny get her computer back? Find out in a hilariously moving musical about negotiation and compromise, based on the Caldecott Honor Book by Doreen Cronin and Betsy Lewin.
Approximate Running Time: 50 Minutes
Recommended Ages: Pre-K through 4
