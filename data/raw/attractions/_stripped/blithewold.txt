Blithewold Manor • Gardens • Arboretum - An American Garden Treasure

Blithewold is a 33-acre summer estate with grand views of Narragansett Bay and is nationally significant in American history as one of the most fully developed and authentic examples of the Country Place Era. The property features a 45-room mansion filled with family heirlooms, and is framed by a series of lovely gardens that range in character from mysterious to exotic and from poetic to practical. An exceptional collection of rare and unusual plants, specimen trees, an accessible greenhouse, and whimsical stonework project a character that is romantic, fresh, and inspiring – and unique to Blithewold.

Our mission: To curate, interpret, and preserve the cultural heritage of one of New England’s finest garden estates for the enjoyment and education of the public.

Come and experience Blithewold today.

Learn About General Admission Tickets

Where can I buy general admission?
You can buy general admission at the Welcome Center when you arrive. This is the building next to the parking lot. You need to buy general admission to access the Manor and grounds.

Ticket Pricing

Adult (age 17 and up) – $22
Child (age 6 – 10) – $6
Youth (age 11 – 16) – $11
Family (2 adults and 2 children) – $50
Military and Student – $18
Cyclist – $14
SNAP/EBT – $2

Blithewold Members, Reciprocal Gardens, NARM, and Children under 5 – Free

What is included with general admission?
With general admission, you can tour the Manor and grounds. It does NOT include programming, like Sparkle, Afternoon Teas, outdoor concerts, etc.

When is the last admission?
Last admission at 3 PM.

Can I buy tickets online?
We’ve recently discontinued selling online admission. It was no longer necessary.

If I bought my tickets online, before 5/5/26, can I still use them?
Tickets bought online before 5/5/26, can be redeemed at the Welcome Center until 5/31/27.

What are the hours for the Manor and grounds?
You can look at the hours: HERE

What if I am part of a reciprocal program, like NARM?
You can check in at the Welcome Center with your reciprocal membership card from your chosen organization ready to show. If you are unsure if your membership is reciprocal we suggest checking with that organization (the home institution).

Do you offer SNAP/EBT pricing?
Yes we do. You can buy those tickets in the Welcome Center. When checking in please have your card ready to show.

Is this a guided tour?
No, it’s a self-guided tour. But you can download an audio tour on your phone for a more guided experience when you arrive. You can look at the tour page HERE if you are interested in a guided tour experience.

Blithewold Manor, Gardens & Arboretum offers a refined and romantic setting for weddings of timeless elegance. Set on 33 acres overlooking Narragansett Bay, the historic estate features a grand manor house, sweeping lawns, century-old specimen trees, and seven meticulously maintained gardens in continuous bloom from May through October.

Couples enjoy exclusive use of the property for a single wedding per day, ensuring privacy, tranquility, and an unforgettable guest experience.

Blithewold offers a rare combination of historic grandeur, natural beauty, and thoughtful hospitality. The perfect setting for a truly memorable celebration.

The Setting

Great Lawn Wedding Tent: A 40’ x 100’ elegant tent with clear sides, permanent slate flooring, and specialty lighting, accommodating up to 180 guests. The tent is positioned on the 10-acre Great Lawn with sweeping bay views and spectacular sunsets.

Historic Manor Access: Exclusive use of the mansion’s first floor during the cocktail hour.

Blithewold's Mission is to preserve New England’s finest garden estate through excellence in horticulture and historic preservation, and by our example to teach and inspire others.

The architectural and landscape features that form Blithewold’s character have remained largely unchanged since its period of significance (1894 – 1930). At the same time, both buildings and gardens have evolved in subtle ways in response to the transition from private to public landscape, as well as to hurricanes and weather events, and longer-term phenomena such as the growth and maturation of trees.

Blithewold Mansion, Gardens and Arboretum is widely recognized as a thoughtfully conserved and authentic historic site of consequence; a cultural landscape of historical importance and contemporary relevance; and a place of tranquility, beauty, and repose. Blithewold respects John DeWolf’s vision of creating “an estate in which new beauties are constantly revealed and the perfect accord between Architecture and Grounds is ever apparent.”

Blithewold’s stewards preserve the property in accordance with its cultural and environmental significance while maintaining operational excellence and integrity. The property is fully supported by the greater community, its membership, donors, visitors, scholars, and staff.

Blithewold’s heritage assets are multiple, varied, and definable in overlapping individual categories of artifactual material and botanical culture. These physical attributes are closely networked intellectually, and with the property’s social history, provide the foundation for Blithewold’s significance as a historic site.

We are dedicated to preserving Blithewold's unique and original history. We invite you to learn more about our efforts to preserve our historical grounds and buildings of architectural significance.
