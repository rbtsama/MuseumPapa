The Trustees of Reservations is here to protect and share the Massachusetts places people love for their exceptional scenic, historic, and ecological value. For their trails, their history, their gardens, their beaches. For the simple reason that nature and culture can soothe the soul and improve our lives.

Levels and Benefits of Membership

When you join The Trustees of Reservations, you gain access to our more than 120 reservations, thousands of programs and events, and much more!

Free or reduced admission (including parking) to our more than 120 reservations, including Crane Beach , deCordova Sculpture Park and Museum , the Old Manse , World’s End , Rocky Woods , Cape Poge , and Notchview .

Members’ advance ticketing and Members-only days for select events and programs, including Winterlights.

A year’s subscription to Special Places , our Member magazine. Here you’ll find engaging articles on the people and places of Massachusetts, a regional round-up of programs and events, and more.

Great discounts on our unique bed & breakfasts and campgrounds .

Discounts on thousands of events and programs across the state, from guided walks, lectures, and concerts to special programs just for kids.

10% discount at our retail stores and select equipment rentals. Special discount shopping days throughout the year.

Discount on children’s summer camp registration with multiple camps across the state and islands.

Opportunity to purchase a season pass for cross-country skiing at Notchview at our premier cross-country skiing facility in Windsor, MA.

Trustees Members at the $140 Contributing level and above enjoy free admission—and additional benefits—to thousands of participating North American Reciprocal Museum network (NARM) and International National Trust Organization (INTO) institutions worldwide.

Overview

A historic dike once connected Eagle Island to the mainland so livestock could graze the island’s pastures. Today, wandering the gently rolling fields and marsh meadows means encountering bobolinks and other wildlife that cluster around this placid island, located in one of the least disturbed parts of the entire 25,000-acre Great Marsh. The backside of the island overlooks extensive open marsh drained by Labor-in-Vain Creek, a remote landscape isolated from the modern world. Watch wading birds such as glossy ibis and snowy and American egrets hunt for fish in this.

Ideas for Your Visit

A one-mile loop trail circles a landscape covered by a forest of white pine, oak, and hickory, revealing the remnants of stone walls that were built along its perimeter

Regulations & Advisories

Dogs are allowed on-leash only.

Authorized seasonal bow hunting is allowed on this reservation for a limited number of hunters through a deer management program administered by The Trustees. Per MassWildlife regulations, hunting is permitted from the first Monday in October through December each year, from ½ hour before sunrise all day until ½ hour after sunset, Monday through Saturday. Hunting is not allowed on Sundays. Signage is posted at the property listing safety precautions and requirements. Learn more about hunting on Trustees properties . Any questions may be directed to The Trustees at castlehill@thetrustees.org .

PHOTOGRAPHY : We ask that photographers or their clients become Contributing Level Members before conducting portrait sessions at this property. Careers

Join The Trustees!

We love Massachusetts’ special places and, since you’re here, we bet you do too. Joining The Trustees is not just a job, it is a commitment to protecting the irreplaceable treasures under our care. We provide powerful connections to nature and culture for residents and visitors, and in doing so we are improving quality of life across the Commonwealth. We’re glad you’re interested in joining us in this important and inspiring work! Reach out to us at people@thetrustees.org

Job Openings

Looking for rewarding, mission-driven work in the land conservation and preservation space? Look through our current job openings and see if you’re a fit.

Our Benefits

In support of our staff and their families, we provide comprehensive top-notch benefits and an array of special perks. Full-time, year-round staff can enjoy including: Medical, dental, and vision insurance with employer premium contributions ranging from 75%-85%; traditional and Roth 401(k) plan with 5% employer match after one year of employment; employer paid life and disability plans; paid vacation, sick and personal time (thirteen paid holidays, eight early release Fridays); Day of Service to travel to another site to work in a group project; Day of Wonder to explore one or more Trustees sites on your own or with colleagues; complimentary Member-level access and discounts and 50% discount for Trustees summer camps; reciprocity program offering free and discounted admissions and memberships to more than 50 Massachusetts museums and cultural institutions...and much more!

Our Workplace

At The Trustees, we aim to create a thriving, inclusive workplace that values each member of our team. We aspire to reflect and effectively serve the residents and communities of Massachusetts, who are at the core of our mission. We offer full-time, part-time, and seasonal positions. Whether you’ve lived in Massachusetts for years, have just relocated, or are here for only a few months; whether you’re in Boston, Stockbridge, or somewhere in between; whether you’re an event planner, a curator, an ecologist, or have a variety of other skills, we have an opportunity for you! When you work for The Trustees, you become part of a dedicated and passionate team.

Diversity at The Trustees

Our commitment to Diversity, Belonging, Inclusion, and Equity (DBIE) is deeply grounded in the Trustees’ mission to protect and share Massachusetts’ iconic places – for the simple reason that nature and culture can soothe the soul and improve our lives. Not for some. But for everyone. Forever.

We ask them every single year and they say: "Feeling connected to and inspired by a meaningful mission," "Working with colleagues who are committed, talented, knowledgeable, and supportive, and who care about one another’s success," "Working in unique and beautiful places with rich histories," "Experiencing joy in delighting visitors with meaningful experiences," "Enjoying the variety found in day-to-day work at an organization that is thriving and growing," and "Appreciating the opportunities for creativity and freedom to explore new ideas."
