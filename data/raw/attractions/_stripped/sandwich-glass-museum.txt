Visit

Since 1925 the Sandwich Glass Museum has been interpreting the glassmaking history of the Town of Sandwich, and since 1980 we have been actively fostering contemporary glass art and the artists who make it. Our extensive galleries take you on a journey through centuries of glass making from pre-history to the present day.

Why Visit The Sandwich Glass Museum

Nestled in the very heart of scenic Sandwich Massachusetts, the first town on Cape Cod, the Sandwich Glass Museum is a must-see attraction for all visitors to Cape Cod.
There are so many things to do : watch a live demonstration of glass blowing, offered every hour on the hour; watch an educational video about the glass factory that brought prosperity to the Town of Sandwich in the nineteenth century; stroll through the permanent collection galleries that wind through the our genuinely antique building; learn about the science and chemistry of glass making; admire the beauty and variety of pressed and hand blown glass of yesteryear, marvel at the creative genius of contemporary glass artisans whose work is on display in our contemporary galleries, and last but not least, browse our fabulous museum gift shop for a unique handmade souvenir of your visit.
The best attractions are the ones that offer the visitor a unique perspective, and the Sandwich Glass Museum is the perfect place to spend several hours immersed in the past and present world of glass manufacturing and studio artistry.
The Experience of a Lifetime! Once you’ve walked through the galleries and browsed the gift shop, come back for your own “ Glass Experience ”, in which our talented glass demonstration artists show you how to make your very own handmade glass souvenir. “Make Glass Art and Memories” - enjoy the thrill of handling molten glass, then treasure the product of your creative endeavor as a lasting keepsake of this fascinating and adventurous experience.
Sandwich: The Town that Glass Built. Did you know that there are dozens of contemporary studio glass artists currently creating glass art in New England? On Cape Cod alone we have half a dozen hot shops and even more flameworkers. The mission of the Sandwich Glass Museum includes supporting new generations of artists and enthusiasts by carrying the work of contemporary glass artists in our museum gift shop. Every purchase you make helps to support both the museum and the glass artist who created the piece. Help keep the art of glass blowing alive by making a purchase when you come visit, or browse our online gift shop .

Access for All

Museum Mondays in May
The Sandwich Glass Museum is delighted to participate in the Cape Cod Museum Trail's Museum Mondays in May program. Each year we offer free admission to all on a designated Monday in May. Watch our website and social media for this year's date!

Learn More

Blue Star Museum
The Sandwich Glass Museum offers free admission to active military personnel year-round, and for active military personnel and their families from Armed Forces Day through Labor Day.

Learn More

Card to Culture Discount
The Sandwich Glass Museum is proud to participate in the Card to Culture program, a collaboration between Mass Cultural Council and the Department of Transitional Assistance , the Women, Infants & Children (WIC) Nutrition Program , and the Massachusetts Health Connector , designed to broaden accessibility to cultural programming.
EBT, SunBucks , WIC , and ConnectorCare cardholders can purchase discounted admission tickets for $5 per person; children 14 and under are free.

Bus tours and group rates

We are happy to offer discount admission prices to groups of 10 persons or more.

To arrange your group's visit, please email tourbooking@sandwichglassmuseum.org

Accessibility

The Museum is handicap accessible.

There are clearly marked spaces reserved for handicapped parking

The front entrance has a wheelchair ramp

The museum is all on one level with no interior stairs or ramps

Restrooms are wheelchair accessible

We welcome legally registered service animals. For service animal guidelines in Massachusetts, please click here .

For more information about our building and accessibility please contact our Accessibility Coordinator at 508-888-0251 or email the museum at info@sandwichglassmuseum.org

Gift Shop

The Sandwich Glass Museum's Gift Shop is located just inside the main doors to the right, prior to reaching the admissions desk. The Gift Shop is free to visit, and is open to the public during regular Museum business hours.

Planning your stay

The Sandwich Chamber of Commerce has an informative site with suggestions on where to stay and dine while visiting the town of Sandwich.

Directions

The Sandwich Glass Museum is located at 129 Main Street, Sandwich, MA 02563, opposite Old Town Hall. The parking lot and entrance are located just around the corner on Tupper Road.

From the Mid-Cape Highway (Rte.6):

Take exit 59 for MA-130 N toward Sandwich

Turn left onto MA-130 N from the highway exit ramp

Follow MA-130 for 1.4 miles (about 3 minutes)

You will see the Glass Museum on the right hand side. Drive past the museum and turn right onto Tupper Road

The parking lot and museum main entrance will be on your right immediately after turning onto Tupper Road

From Rte. 28:

From the Sagamore Bridge/Bourne take Route 6A East to Route 130 (turn right) – Continue on 130 South for about 3 miles. The Museum will be on your left directly opposite the Old Town Hall – at the corner of 130 and Tupper Road. Entrance is located on Tupper Road.

From Boston and points north:

Take Rte. 3 south across the Sagamore Bridge. Route 3 becomes Rte. 6 at the bridge. Follow the directions from the Mid-Cape Highway. Allow one hour from downtown Boston in normal traffic.

From Providence and points west:

Take Interstate 195 to its termination at Rte. 25 and follow the signs to Cape Cod. Cross the Bourne Bridge and circle the rotary to the third road (Sandwich Road) and head north along the canal. Follow the signs to Rte. 6 just before the Sagamore Bridge. Follow the directions from the Mid-Cape Highway. Allow one hour from Providence and 30 minutes from New Bedford.

Marvel With Us

Glass making is an ancient and beautiful art form. Come experience the endless variation of color and form that has mesmerized glass makers and artists for millennia. We invite you to embark on a journey through time from pre-history to the present day as we narrate and celebrate the story of glass making in Sandwich, MA.

Hours & Admission

April 1st through Dec 30th we are open daily from 9:30 - 5, except for Thanksgiving and Christmas. Admission closes at 4:30. In Feb & March we are open Wed - Sun 9:30 - 4, and admission closes at 3. In January we are open by appointment only.
Adult admission to the Museum's galleries and to see a glassblowing demonstration is $15. See Plan Your Visit for the full schedule of admission fees as well as other helpful info regarding accessibility, parking, seasonal hours, etc.
Visiting the Gift Shop is free and open to the public with no admission fee.

Plan Your Visit

Exhibitions

In addition to our permanent collection we have several galleries devoted to special exhibitions that change seasonally. We are currently celebrating Sandwich painter Dodge Macknight in our Center Gallery through March 31st.

Learn More

Special Events

We are continually hosting special events, so check our calendar frequently! In 2025 we're celebrating the bi-centennial of glassmaking in Sandwich, so our event themes are "Illuminating the Past, Sparking the Future".

Events Calendar

Glassblowing Demonstrations

Live 20 minute demonstrations of the art of glassblowing take place every hour on the hour, starting at 10 am.

Learn More

Museum Gift Shop

Our spectacular Museum Gift Shop features contemporary hand blown glass made on site by our demo glassblowers and by other American glass artists. Much of the art we carry was made locally, as dozens of glassblowers have made Cape Cod and New England their home.
Admission to the gift shop is free.

Museum Galleries

From ancient Egypt to the present day, learn the history of glassmaking, and of the 19th century glass factory in Sandwich. Then apply your newfound historical knowledge to enhance your appreciation of the glass art on display in the Contemporary Gallery and Museum Gift Shop.
