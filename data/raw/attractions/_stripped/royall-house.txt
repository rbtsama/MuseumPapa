Welcome to the Royall House and Slave Quarters

The Royall House and Slave Quarters functions as a site of memory. In the eighteenth century, the plantation was home to the Royalls, the largest slaveholding family in Massachusetts, and at least sixty enslaved women, men, and children whose forced labor helped build the Royall family’s wealth. As one of the only remaining freestanding quarters where enslaved people lived and worked in the North, the museum bears witness to their lives, to the intertwined stories of wealth and bondage in pre-Revolutionary Massachusetts, and to the resistance and political and legal activism of enslaved and free Black people in the eighteenth century.

The museum is open to the public for tours on weekends from June through October, while school and group tours are available from mid-March to mid-November. The museum also presents a range of public programs throughout the year. Members receive free admission to the museum and free or discounted admission to most programs.

Museums Are Not Neutral

As people across the world take to the streets to protest the police killings of Breonna Taylor in Louisville, George Floyd in Minneapolis, and Tony McDade in Tallahassee, we have a responsibility to speak out.

We are committed to telling the history of slavery and freedom while highlighting how the legacy of enslavement creates systemic inequalities today. Thus, we stand in solidarity with the protesters bravely fighting against police violence and demanding justice, accountability, and a transformation of policies that sustain racial and economic inequalities.

Our job is not only to listen, but also to uplift and amplify the history and voices of Black people locally, nationally, and globally in the struggle for freedom.

Museums Are Not Neutral. Black Lives Matter.

Directions and Map

The Royall House and Slave Quarters site is located at 15 George Street in Medford, Massachusetts, just outside of Medford Square, near the intersection with Main Street and next to Royall Park.

Maps

Here is a MapQuest Link . Here is a Google Maps link .

Directions by Car

Take exit 32 off Route 93. Proceed on Route 60 West (Salem Street) to second light (Medford Square). Turn left to enter Main Street going South. You will go over the Mystic River and under an overpass. After about a block, bear right at the Mobil Station to stay on Main Street, and turn right at the blinking light onto George Street (there is a park with a brick/iron fence at that corner). Proceed one half block to the museum. Free on-street parking is available on George Street and other nearby streets; please obey signs regarding resident permit restrictions.

Public Transportation

Take the MBTA Green Line Extension Eastbound to Medford/Tufts station, then walk 0.4 miles to the museum. With the Joyce Cummings Center on your left, walk north on College Avenue to the intersection of George Street, turn right, and arrive at the Royall House and Slave Quarters on the left.

Take the MBTA Bus 96 from Harvard Square or Davis Square (both on the Red Line) to the “George Street and Main Street” stop: MBTA Bus 96 Schedule . The Royall House and Slave Quarters is visible behind Royall Park on George Street.

Take the MBTA Bus 101 from Malden Center or Sullivan Square (both on the Orange Line) to the “George Street and Main Street” stop: MBTA Bus 101 Schedule .

Hours and Admission

Hours

The museum is open from 1:00-4:00 pm Saturday and Sunday from June 6 – November 15.

Tours of the mansion are guided and begin at the top of the hour. Admission to the Slave Quarters, which also houses the archaeological exhibit, gift shop, and restrooms, is free of charge.

Group Tours

During our tour season, no reservation is required for groups of fewer than 10 people. Groups of 10 or more are considered group tours and must have a reservation. Visit our Group Tours page for group rates and scheduling. Visit our School Groups page for information regarding school group tours.

Admission

Admission is always FREE for Royall House and Slave Quarters members. Learn how to join.

We offer free admission to active duty military personnel and their families. Blue Star Museums is a program that offers free admission to museums for all active duty, National Guard, and Reserve military personnel and their families from Memorial Day through Labor Day.

We offer free admission to current or retired members of the Massachusetts Teachers Association upon presentation of a valid MTA card.

We offer discounted admission to EBT and WIC cardholders through the Mass Cultural Council’s Card to Culture program , offered in collaboration with the Department of Transitional Assistance, the WIC Nutrition Program, and the Massachusetts Health Connector. See the complete lists of participating organizations offering EBT , WIC , and ConnectorCare discounts.

Photography

Noncommercial still photography is permitted inside the Royall mansion and the Slave Quarters as well as on the grounds as long as it does not interfere with tours or other programs. “Selfie sticks,” tripods, and other similar accessories are not permitted indoors. Filming/videotaping is not allowed without specific prior permission.

Visit

Royall House and Slave Quarters site is located at 15 George Street in Medford, Massachusetts, just outside Medford Square, near the intersection with Main Street and next to the Royall Park.

Here is a MapQuest Link . Here is a Google Maps Link .

Our museum’s regular 2024 tour season has concluded for the year. Please check back here or follow our social media for updates on next year’s tour season, which spans Saturday, June 7, to Sunday, October 12.

Directions by Car

Take exit 23 off Route 93. Proceed on Route 60 West (Salem Street) to second light (Medford Square). Stay to the left. Bear left to enter Main Street going South. (You will go over the Mystic River and under an overpass.) Bear right at the Mobil Station to stay on Main Street, and turn right at the blinking light onto George Street. Proceed one half block to the museum. Free on-street parking is available on George Street and other nearby streets; please obey signs regarding resident permit restrictions.

Public Transportation

Take the MBTA Green Line Extension Eastbound to Medford/Tufts station, then walk 0.4 miles to the museum. With the Joyce Cummings Center on your left, walk north on College Avenue to the intersection of George Street, turn right, and arrive at the Royall House and Slave Quarters on the left.

Take the MBTA Bus 96 from Harvard, Porter, or Davis Square (Red Line) to the “George Street and Main Street” stop: MBTA Bus 96 Schedule . From Medford Square, the 96 stops on George Street next to the Royall House. The Royall House and Slave Quarters is visible behind Royall Park on George Street.

Take the MBTA Bus 101 from Malden Center or Sullivan Square (Orange Line) to the “George Street and Main Street” stop: MBTA Bus 101 Schedule . The Royall House and Slave Quarters is visible behind Royall Park on George Street.

Admission and Hours

For information on our admissions, tickets, and when our site is available to visit, please visit our Hours and Admissions Page .

Accessibility

The first floors of the Slave Quarters and the Royall House are wheelchair accessible. Inside the Slave Quarters is a restroom with grab bars. However, there are only stairs going up to the second floor of the Royall House. There is no elevator on-site due to the unaltered historic nature of the property.
