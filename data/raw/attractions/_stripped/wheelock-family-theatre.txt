Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a ticket for my child?

Yes—everyone attending a performance, regardless of age, must have a ticket.

What is youth pricing?

Youth pricing is available for audiences ages 3–18 across all seating tiers, making it easier for families to attend together.

Are ticket prices inclusive of fees?

Yes. All ticket prices are all-inclusive , meaning the price you see is the final price—no additional fees at checkout.

How do I use a discount or promo code?

After clicking “Purchase Tickets,” you’ll be taken to the performance calendar to select your desired date and time. Once you reach the seating map, click “Have a promotion code?” in the upper right corner, enter your code, and select “Apply” —your ticket prices will update automatically.

If you need assistance at any point, our Box Office team is happy to help.

What discounts are available?

We offer a variety of discounts, including youth pricing, group rates, community programs like Card to Culture, and special offers for students, educators, and the Boston University community. Visit our pricing section above for full details.

Can I exchange my tickets?

Yes! Ticket exchanges are available based on availability. Season Members receive free exchanges as part of their benefits.

Do you offer refunds?

Tickets are generally non-refundable. If your plans change, we encourage you to exchange your tickets for another performance.

What if I purchased tickets through a third-party site?

Tickets purchased through third-party vendors cannot be refunded or exchanged through the Wheelock Family Theatre Box Office. Please contact the original seller directly.

Questions?

Send us an email at wfttix@bu.edu or call our box office, 617-353-3001.

Directions & Parking

Get here with MBTA

Wheelock Family Theatre is a five-minute walk from both the Fenway or Longwood MBTA stops on the Green Riverside line (D train) .

MBTA bus stops located on Brookline Avenue and in the Longwood/Hospital area are only a few short minutes away.

Driving Directions

Located in Boston’s Fenway neighborhood, Wheelock Family Theatre is easily accessible from Rt. 9, Rt. 2 , and Storrow Drive .

The theatre is located on the Riverway, between the intersections of Brookline Ave and Longwood Ave.

Parking Information

Limited on-street parking is available in front of the theatre, please obey all posted signage.

Please note: “Live Parking” means that someone is sitting in the vehicle at all times while it is parked.

Wheelock Family Theatre patrons and guests can find parking at the MASCO garage located at 375 Longwood Avenue , between Brookline Avenue (Longwood Medical Area) and The Riverway. The garage is located behind Temple Israel and is only a short walk to the front doors of Wheelock Family Theatre. Please visit the MASCO website for more information on current discounted evening and weekend rates. There is no validation necessary for discounted parking at the MASCO garage.

Handicapped Parking
There are a limited number of handicapped designated parking spaces located on the Riverway in front of the theatre. Additional accessible parking can be found at the MASCO garage located at 375 Longwood Avenue. Please visit the MASCO website for more information.

Uber/Lyft/Ride Shares

Use our street address of 180 Riverway Boston 02215 to have your car service either drop you and your guests off at the theatre or for pick-ups.

Theatre for Every Family

A visit to Wheelock Family Theatre is more than a performance—it’s a shared experience for theatregoers of all ages. From planning your trip to exploring the neighborhood to settling into your seats, we’re here to help you make the most of your time with us.

Getting Here

Wheelock Family Theatre
180 Riverway, Boston, MA 02215

Public Transportation

Wheelock Family Theatre is easily accessible by public transit:

Green Line (D or E branches) – Fenway / Longwood stops

Several MBTA bus routes serve the Longwood and Fenway area

Public transportation is a great option for families and groups visiting from across Greater Boston.

Driving & Parking

There is no on-site parking, but several nearby garages are available:

MASCO Garage – 375 Longwood Ave

Landmark Center Garage – 401 Park Dr

Pilgrim Road Garage – 170 Pilgrim Rd

Parking is available at market rates and is not validated.

We recommend allowing extra time for parking, especially on weekends.

Prepare for Your Visit

Wheelock Family Theatre is designed with children and families in mind, and we strive to create a welcoming, comfortable experience for all audiences. Hover over the boxes below to prepare for your visit to the theatre.

Social Stories

Resources to Prepare for Your Visit

We offer virtual social stories to help audiences understand what to expect when visiting the theatre—from entering the building to watching the performance. These tools are especially helpful for first-time visitors and audiences who benefit from additional preparation.

Accessibility

We’re committed to creating an inclusive experience for every audience member—explore our accessibility services and supports to help you plan your visit with confidence.

Concessions

A selection of pre-packaged snacks and beverages is available in the lobby—perfect for a treat before the show or at intermission, with cash and credit accepted.

When should we arrive?

Theatre lobby doors open approximately 60 minutes before curtain with theatre house doors opening 30-minutes before the start of the show

We recommend arriving 30–45 minutes early to allow time for seating and getting settled

All guests must have a ticket to enter the theatre

A view from the stage in the theatre.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Wheelock Family Theatre located?

Wheelock Family Theatre at Boston University is located at 180 Riverway, Boston, MA 02215 , in Boston’s Fenway neighborhood.

Where can I park?

While there is no on-site parking, several nearby garages are available:

MASCO Garage – 375 Longwood Ave

Landmark Center – 401 Park Dr

Pilgrim Road Garage – 170 Pilgrim Rd

Parking is at market rates and is not validated.

Is the theatre accessible by public transportation?

Yes—Wheelock Family Theatre is accessible via public transportation, including nearby Green Line stops in the Fenway/Longwood area. We encourage you to utilize online maps to identify the best route for your visit.

When should I arrive?

We recommend arriving 30–45 minutes before your performance to allow time for parking, seating, and getting settled before the show begins.

Is food and drink available at the theatre?

Yes! A selection of pre-packaged food and drink is available for purchase before the performance and during intermission. We accept cash or credit card payments.

What should we bring?

We recommend bringing your tickets (digital or printed) and a light layer, as the theatre can be cool.

What accessibility services are available?

We offer a range of accessibility features, including open captioning at every performance, accessible seating, and select ASL-interpreted and audio-described performances. Social stories and sensory supports are also available.

What should I do if I need accommodations?

Please contact our Box Office (617-353-3001 or wfttix@bu.edu) in advance so we can ensure your visit is as comfortable and accessible as possible.

Are there things to do nearby?

Yes! The Fenway neighborhood offers parks, green spaces, and family-friendly dining options, making it easy to turn your visit into a full day experience.

Questions?

Send us an email at wfttix@bu.edu or call our box office, 617-353-3001.
