What is America's Stonehenge?

America's Stonehenge is a historic site and museum that preserves more than 4,000 years of human history, right here in Salem, New Hampshire. From the age of ancient construction to Pawtucket occupation, the colonial period, the Pattee era, and later archaeological research, many people and cultures have lived and worked on this site. Their stories are captured in the artifacts, structures, and landscapes that they left behind.

​

Today, you can visit America's Stonehenge to explore its historic structures, ancient landscape, and active archaeological sites. Your visitation allows the museum to continue its research and preservation efforts, recovering the lives of our ancestors and sharing their knowledge with the future.

Experience Ancient History

Walking the half-mile nature trail is like traveling through time. Your path will lead you from the modern day through the early excavations of the site, the Pattee era, and colonial period, until you reach the ancient site that is America's Stonehenge. When you reach the summit of Mystery Hill, you will be surrounded by a complex of megalithic structures, standing stones, artistic walls, and astronomical alignments - truly one of America's most fascinating ancient mysteries. Exploring the site will likely yield more questions than answers, but a chance to journey through the past and surround yourself with these ancient monuments is well worth it. As you return to the Visitor Center, you will have a better appreciation for this ancient landscape and the people who once lived here many generations ago.

Self-Guided Tours

Explore the site at your own pace with a paper map, or download our free mobile app and audio tour for an immersive experience. Well-marked and maintained trails are easy to follow to and from the historic site.

Visitor Center and Museum

The Visitor Center, located at the base of Mystery Hill, houses all of the site's amenities including the parking lot, admission counter, restroom facilities, and gift shop. The on-site museum is also located here, providing an opportunity to view artifacts collected from the site and modern archaeological equipment that is used in the field.

Accessibility

The loop trail to the historic site is a half mile in total, and usually takes about one hour to complete. The trail is steadily sloping with uneven terrain, including several steps, and may be difficult for individuals with limited mobility. Low-heeled, supportive footwear is recommended.

​

​The Visitor Center is accessible, with ample space in the parking lot for vehicles of all sizes including vans, buses, RVs, and trailers, as well as designated handicap parking.

VIP Tour Information

Pet Policy

To limit our impact on wildlife and preserve the historic structures, pets are not allowed on the site.

​

Service animals trained to perform an essential service are allowed.

Photo Policy

Personal photography, video, and audio recording is allowed on the site and in the museum. Please be respectful of individual privacy. Commercial photography is subject to our media policy.

Drone Use

Personal drones are not permitted on the site, and may not be flown over the site. Commercial drones for research or filming may be used after receiving permission from America's Stonehenge management.
