Hours

We recommend you purchase tickets in advance to ensure entry. Walk-in visits to SSC are subject to availability.

Please refer to the web calendar for a complete, monthly overview of the seasonal schedule and hours.

Member-only Hour: Saturdays, 9-10am, June 6–August 29, 2026 (Not offered on Saturday, June 20, 2026 when the Center hosts its World Ocean Day celebration.)
2026 Holiday Schedule: closed Easter, closed Thanksgiving Day, closed Christmas Eve, closed Christmas Day, closed New Year’s Day

About Your Visit

Members should reserve tickets to visit SSC and OPSP in advance; member OPSP only visits, please also register in advance and show your membership card (digital or paper) at the Park gate.
Advance tickets for general admission to SSC are available and recommended; walk-ins are subject to availability.
You may visit the Park before or after visiting SSC, or anytime during its open hours on the date of your ticket.
Non-members must pay an entry fee to Odiorne Point State Park (payable when purchasing SSC admission tickets online).
Walk-in visits to Seacoast Science Center are subject to availability.

Visitor Code of Conduct

Seacoast Science Center is a place for discovery and exploration, and your safety is our number one priority. We are a public space that welcomes visitors of all ages and abilities. We ask all visitors to follow these guidelines to be a part of creating a welcoming learning environment.
Ask questions! Our staff and volunteers love sharing information about our animals and exhibits, and we appreciate learning about what is important to you. Be aware of your surroundings. We are a busy space with touch tanks and hands-on exhibits. Please be patient and be kind to other visitors. Be understanding. Many of our visitors are children who are in an exciting, engaging setting and energy can be high.
By entering Seacoast Science Center, you are agreeing to the following guidelines for visitor conduct:
Treat fellow visitors, volunteers, and staff with respect.
Stay home if you are not feeling well.
Handle our animals gently.
Provide adult supervision to your children throughout your visit.
Have fun!

We are a Blue Star Museum!

Seacoast Science Center is a proud participant of Blue Star Museums , a program offering free admission to active duty military personnel and their families from Armed Forces Day, May 16, 2026, through Labor Day, September 7, 2026. The free admission program is available for those currently serving in the United States Military –Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Space Force, Coast Guard–including Reservists, National Guardsman (regardless of status), U.S. Public Health Commissioned Corps, NOAA Commissioned Corps, and up to five family members. To make reservations in advance, click the General Admission button; choose your date, time of visit, and the number of guests. Then click “add promo or discount code” and enter the code “BLUESTAR.” This will deduct Seacoast Science Center admission from your total. You are responsible for paying the Odiorne Point State Park fee. Military ID must be shown at the admissions desk when you arrive. Find more information about this program on The National Endowment for the Arts .

EBT, SNAP, and WIC Card Holders

Present your card and pay $2 per person for admission to Seacoast Science Center. Discounted admission is valid for individuals and families (up to 4 individuals) presenting an EBT, SNAP, or WIC card. This benefit can be used for pass-holder admission only and cannot be combined with other offers. This benefit does not include discounted admission to Odiorne Point State Park.
To make reservations in advance, click the Pass Holder Admission button; choose your date, time of visit, and the number of guests. Click Choose an Option for each ticket and select Museums for All Pass. Walk-ins are welcome but are subject to availability.

Daily Visitor Programs

During your visit to Seacoast Science Center and Odiorne Point State Park, join our knowledgeable team of naturalists for daily visitor programs to learn about the wonders of our coast, its wildlife and the animals at SSC.

Welcome to the Seacoast Science Center!

Located in Odiorne Point State Park
Every day here is a new adventure in learning! Located along the New Hampshire coast within Odiorne Point State Park, we provide a range of engaging daily visitor programs that are included with your admission. Immerse yourself in the wonders of our coastline through interactive exhibits and live animal presentations, or delve into the rich history and ecology of our unique coastal environment with guided walks and hands-on activities. Whether you’re a curious child or an inquisitive adult, our programs are crafted to ignite your imagination and nurture a lifelong passion for science, nature, and the ocean. Join us for a day of exploration, and let the Center and our seaside park on the New Hampshire coastline be your classroom!
Daily Visitor Programming
During your visit to the Seacoast Science Center, be sure to join our team of Naturalists for our daily visitor programs! These activities may include outdoor adventures, meet-and-greets with our animals, feeding sessions, or community science experiments.
Program Schedule*: Most days, programs are offered at 11:00 a.m. and 2:00 p.m. , with a feeding time at 1:00 p.m.
Programming related to holidays or specific events, can be found on our web calendar .

*Please note that all programs and times are subject to change, and additional programs may be added daily. When you arrive at the Center, check the whiteboard at the entrance for the day’s scheduled programs.

Book Your Visit!

Some of SSC's Visitor Program Offerings:

Turtle Time
Spend time with our resident three-toed box turtle and discover why he’s named Raspberry! Plus, learn about other turtle species found on land and in the ocean, and conservation efforts to protect these incredible creatures!

All About Whales
Join our Naturalists to learn about the largest animals on the planet. We’ll explore their survival strategies, diets, winter migrations, and more! Don’t miss the chance to see and learn about our resident humpback whale skeleton, Tofu.

Nature Scavenger Hunt
Ready for an adventure? Head outside with our naturalist to find fascinating plants, wildlife, and historical remnants around Odiorne Point State Park. Can you locate everything on your list?

Iconic Crustacean
Attend a meet and greet with the Gulf of Maine’s most famous crustaceans: the American lobster! We will talk about their adaptations and anatomy, including genetic variations that lead to unique shell colors. You will also get the chance to feel their exoskeleton!

Wrackline Walk
Step outside to uncover what the tides have left behind. We’ll stroll along the wrackline—the area where ocean meets shore—to observe seaweeds, shells, driftwood, and more. By exploring the highest point of the tide, we can learn about what is living on the rocky shore and our oceans health.

Feeding Times
It’s lunchtime at SSC! Join our naturalists, aquarists, and animals to learn more about our residents and the care we provide them at the Center. You may encounter our Atlantic lumpfish, lined seahorse, honeycomb moray eel, chain catsharks, and other resident species as they enjoy lunch.

Book Your Visit!

This is just a sneak peek of the exciting daily program offerings available during your visit to the Seacoast Science Center in Odiorne Point State Park.
All programs are FREE with admission and are suitable for all ages.
Be sure to check our program whiteboard at the entrance when you arrive to see what’s scheduled for the day.
For special programming related to holidays or specific events, details will be posted on our web calendar .
