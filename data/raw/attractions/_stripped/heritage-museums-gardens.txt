Discover Heritage Museums & Gardens:

Art, History, and Nature

Become A Member

Becoming a member at Heritage connects you to a community of people who love to learn, explore, play and engage. As a member, you’ll have access to FREE unlimited admission, invitations to special events, early access to the gardens and more!

JOin today!

2026 Exhibits

Cranberries: From Cape Cod to American (April 25 - October 18, 2026)

Whether covering our local bogs or garnishing our cocktails, cranberries have held an ever-present role in Cape Cod life for generations. Come explore the rise of this humble berry through Cape Cod’s history, from its historic use by the Wampanoag people to its modern sale worldwide by companies like Ocean Spray. This exhibition continues the debut of Heritage’s new cranberry collection, the largest museum collection of cranberry history in the country.

LEARN MORE

Remembering the Revolution (April 25 - October 18, 2026)

How do you remember the American Revolution? To mark this year’s anniversary of the signing of the Declaration of Independence, Heritage is unveiling a site-wide exhibition of 250 years of American history. Come explore military dioramas, silver made by Paul Revere, and see ﬁrsthand how the Revolution has shaped the history of southeastern New England.

LEARN MORE

From Carriage to Classic (April 25 - October 18, 2026)

Discover the origin story of the American automobile through 26 cars from Heritage’s collection. Come along for a ride from the late 1800s to the 1960s as the car evolves from a horseless carriage to a streamlined symbol of freedom and independence.

LEARN MORE

Wampanoag Stories: Wetu, Three Sisters-style Garden & Mush8n (April 25 - October 18, 2026)

Throughout the grounds, a Wampanoag wetu, a Three-Sisters-style garden, and a mush8n (pronounced mish-oon) illustrate how the land that is now Heritage Museums & Gardens was likely used as summer planting ﬁelds by local Wampanoag tribes for thousands of years.

LEARN MORE

Our Gardens

Over 100 years ago, Charles Dexter purchased this property, where he began his rhododendron hybridizing work. Because of his passion and dedication, present-day visitors can expect a beautiful display of blooms throughout May into mid-June.

The 2020 expansion of North American Hydrangea Test Garden showcases some of the newest hydrangea cultivars on the market. Many of these cultivars are new to Heritage and will be maturing this season and begin to flower. Additionally, the McInnes Garden will continue the popular pollinator theme, while the Garden of the Senses will feature a newly planted succulent area as well as more herbaceous perennials throughout.

While Here 2026-03-09T13:40:12-04:00

While Here

We do not have an ATM, but both the Lilly’s Café and The Shop at Heritage accept credit and debit cards.

You may bring your own food, water, and soft drinks for a picnic on the grounds. Outdoor seating is first-come, first-served.

Restrooms are wheelchair accessible; family restrooms are available in the in the Barbey Family Welcome Center, the J.K. Lilly III Automobile Gallery, the American Art & Carousel Gallery, and at Lilly’s Café. Portable facilities are also available near the entrance of Hidden Hollow.

Complimentary transportation is available at seven fixed stops throughout the grounds for those with mobility challenges and can be accessed at seven fixed stops throughout the grounds at benches marked with the International Symbol of Access. Transport makes continuous loops approximately every 20 minutes.

Photography is permitted for personal, non-commercial use unless noted by staff or signage. See our photography policy for more information.

In case of a severe weather occurrence during your visit, please follow staff instructions, for your safety. You may be asked to take shelter until bad weather has safely passed.

Restrictions 2026-03-09T13:31:15-04:00

Restrictions

Sorry, pets are not permitted at Heritage. Registered service animals are always welcome throughout Heritage Museums & Gardens. Please be aware, emotional support animals are not considered service animals in the state of Massachusetts. Please click here for information on service animals.
Please do not leave animals in your car in the parking lots.

We are a smoke-free property. The use of tobacco or e-cigarettes is prohibited.

Heritage does not permit firearms on the property.

Heritage has a zero-tolerance policy for verbal or physical harassment, discriminatory or threatening behavior, or interference with the experience of others. We reserve the right to require any person(s) acting inappropriately to leave the grounds.

Do not pick living plants or touch objects on exhibit except where permission is indicated.

Bicycles, skateboards, hoverboards, and similar equipment are prohibited from the grounds.

Visitors are not permitted to bring alcohol onsite; beer and wine may be purchased at Lilly’s Café.

The use of tripods or other photography equipment (including drones) is not permitted on the grounds without approval. View our full photography policy here.

Third-party commercial activity is prohibited on Heritage’s property. To read our policy, click here .

Heritage is home to many plants and animals. Should you encounter any wildlife, please keep a safe distance. Do not attempt to touch or feed the animals.

Heritage is Accessible to All!

Heritage strives to be accessible to visitors of all ages and abilities. Click here to see the ways in which Heritage provides access to visitors.

Thank You for Driving Safely!

Please observe the 20 mph speed limit as you drive through the neighborhoods entering or leaving Heritage.

While Here 2026-03-09T13:40:12-04:00

While Here

We do not have an ATM, but both the Lilly’s Café and The Shop at Heritage accept credit and debit cards.

You may bring your own food, water, and soft drinks for a picnic on the grounds. Outdoor seating is first-come, first-served.

Restrooms are wheelchair accessible; family restrooms are available in the in the Barbey Family Welcome Center, the J.K. Lilly III Automobile Gallery, the American Art & Carousel Gallery, and at Lilly’s Café. Portable facilities are also available near the entrance of Hidden Hollow.

Complimentary transportation is available at seven fixed stops throughout the grounds for those with mobility challenges and can be accessed at seven fixed stops throughout the grounds at benches marked with the International Symbol of Access. Transport makes continuous loops approximately every 20 minutes.

Photography is permitted for personal, non-commercial use unless noted by staff or signage. See our photography policy for more information.

In case of a severe weather occurrence during your visit, please follow staff instructions, for your safety. You may be asked to take shelter until bad weather has safely passed.

Restrictions 2026-03-09T13:31:15-04:00

Restrictions

Sorry, pets are not permitted at Heritage. Registered service animals are always welcome throughout Heritage Museums & Gardens. Please be aware, emotional support animals are not considered service animals in the state of Massachusetts. Please click here for information on service animals.
Please do not leave animals in your car in the parking lots.

We are a smoke-free property. The use of tobacco or e-cigarettes is prohibited.

Heritage does not permit firearms on the property.

Heritage has a zero-tolerance policy for verbal or physical harassment, discriminatory or threatening behavior, or interference with the experience of others. We reserve the right to require any person(s) acting inappropriately to leave the grounds.

Do not pick living plants or touch objects on exhibit except where permission is indicated.

Bicycles, skateboards, hoverboards, and similar equipment are prohibited from the grounds.

Visitors are not permitted to bring alcohol onsite; beer and wine may be purchased at Lilly’s Café.

The use of tripods or other photography equipment (including drones) is not permitted on the grounds without approval. View our full photography policy here.

Third-party commercial activity is prohibited on Heritage’s property. To read our policy, click here .

Heritage is home to many plants and animals. Should you encounter any wildlife, please keep a safe distance. Do not attempt to touch or feed the animals.

Heritage is Accessible to All!

Heritage strives to be accessible to visitors of all ages and abilities. Click here to see the ways in which Heritage provides access to visitors.

Thank You for Driving Safely!

Please observe the 20 mph speed limit as you drive through the neighborhoods entering or leaving Heritage.

Within our peaceful confines you’ll find walking trails, outdoor discovery opportunities, gardens that invite lingering, and exhibits to expand your understanding of art and history. Heritage offers up-close experiences that can’t be found anywhere else. It’s a place of joy and fun. It’s a place to make lasting memories. And it’s a place you’ll want to visit again and again.

Visitor Policies

To learn more about the policies regarding ticketing, cancellations, refunds and weather, please click here .

Guests may purchase General Admission tickets online prior to arrival by clicking here or purchasing at the door upon arrival.

For a list of special events, workshops, and programs click here .

To see our discount programs click here .

Online ticket purchases are strongly encouraged and allow for quick and easy entry.

Tickets are date-specific and are valid for entry any time between 10am-4:30pm.

Tickets are non-refundable. Click here for our ticketing, cancellation, and refund policy.

Heritage is open rain or shine so don’t forget to dress for the elements! Heritage will close in the event of severe weather; please click here for more information.

To reschedule your visit please contact tickets@heritagemuseums.org or call 508-888-3300, ext. 0 prior to your scheduled visit.

Discounts do not apply for select events and programs and cannot be combined with other discounts.

Visitors must be 16 years or older to visit Heritage without an adult.

The Adventure Park at Heritage is NOT included with the purchase of a Heritage Museums & Gardens admission; however, those with a receipt dated within seven days of their visit can bring their Heritage ticket to The Adventure Park to receive a discount – to purchase tickets to The Adventure Park at Heritage click here .

What’s Included with Admission 2026-04-13T10:48:52-04:00

What’s Included with Admission

The J.K. Lilly III Automobile Gallery, featuring the From Carriage to Classic: How Automobiles Transformed America exhibit

The Special Exhibitions Gallery, featuring the Cranberries: From Cape Cod to American Icon exhibit

The American Art & Carousel Gallery, featuring the Heritage Highlights exhibit and rides on the 1908 Looff carousel

The gardens, featuring collections of rhododendrons, hydrangeas, daylilies, and more (seasonal)

Hidden Hollow- an outdoor discovery area for families

Remembering the Revolution exhibit, found throughout the galleries and grounds.

The Wampanoag Stories exhibit featuring a wetu, Three Sisters Garden, and mush8n

While Here 2026-03-09T13:40:12-04:00

While Here

We do not have an ATM, but both the Lilly’s Café and The Shop at Heritage accept credit and debit cards.

You may bring your own food, water, and soft drinks for a picnic on the grounds. Outdoor seating is first-come, first-served.

Restrooms are wheelchair accessible; family restrooms are available in the in the Barbey Family Welcome Center, the J.K. Lilly III Automobile Gallery, the American Art & Carousel Gallery, and at Lilly’s Café. Portable facilities are also available near the entrance of Hidden Hollow.

Complimentary transportation is available at seven fixed stops throughout the grounds for those with mobility challenges and can be accessed at seven fixed stops throughout the grounds at benches marked with the International Symbol of Access. Transport makes continuous loops approximately every 20 minutes.

Photography is permitted for personal, non-commercial use unless noted by staff or signage. See our photography policy for more information.

In case of a severe weather occurrence during your visit, please follow staff instructions, for your safety. You may be asked to take shelter until bad weather has safely passed.

Restrictions 2026-03-09T13:31:15-04:00

Restrictions

Sorry, pets are not permitted at Heritage. Registered service animals are always welcome throughout Heritage Museums & Gardens. Please be aware, emotional support animals are not considered service animals in the state of Massachusetts. Please click here for information on service animals.
Please do not leave animals in your car in the parking lots.

We are a smoke-free property. The use of tobacco or e-cigarettes is prohibited.

Heritage does not permit firearms on the property.

Heritage has a zero-tolerance policy for verbal or physical harassment, discriminatory or threatening behavior, or interference with the experience of others. We reserve the right to require any person(s) acting inappropriately to leave the grounds.

Do not pick living plants or touch objects on exhibit except where permission is indicated.

Bicycles, skateboards, hoverboards, and similar equipment are prohibited from the grounds.

Visitors are not permitted to bring alcohol onsite; beer and wine may be purchased at Lilly’s Café.

The use of tripods or other photography equipment (including drones) is not permitted on the grounds without approval. View our full photography policy here.

Third-party commercial activity is prohibited on Heritage’s property. To read our policy, click here .

Heritage is home to many plants and animals. Should you encounter any wildlife, please keep a safe distance. Do not attempt to touch or feed the animals.

Heritage is Accessible to All!

Heritage strives to be accessible to visitors of all ages and abilities. Click here to see the ways in which Heritage provides access to visitors.

Thank You for Driving Safely!

Please observe the 20 mph speed limit as you drive through the neighborhoods entering or leaving Heritage.
