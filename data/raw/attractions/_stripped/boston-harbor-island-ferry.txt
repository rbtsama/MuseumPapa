The official website of the
Boston Harbor Islands

Made up of 34 islands and peninsulas, Boston Harbor Islands National and State Park is an urban oasis just minutes from Boston’s city center. Hop aboard a park ferry and discover islands rich with opportunities for outdoor recreation, relaxation and learning. Check the park calendar for special events or create your own adventure with our trip planning tools below.

Park News

An historic cleanup effort to preserve the Boston Harbor Islands and its natural treasures
Outer Boston Harbor Island Cleanup
The Boston Harbor Islands are undergoing a once-in-a-generation cleanup to restore their natural beauty and ecological health. Spearheaded by the Massachusetts Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR) and the Center for Coastal Studies (CCS), this initiative focuses on removing significant marine debris from five key islands: Great Brewster, Middle Brewster, Outer Brewster, Calf, and Lovells Islands.

Mini-Grant Program
Innovation Islands
The Innovation Islands program awards grants of up to $5,000 to local community leaders to design, promote, and facilitate programming on and around the Boston Harbor Islands. Learn more about past grant recipients and find out how to apply for a grant for your community.

Access + Hours

Whether you are arriving by ferry or with your own vessel, being aware of seasons and hours for each island location will help ensure an easy and successful trip.

Access and hours vary from one island to the next. Please read the information provided below to plan your next visit.

Most park islands are open to visitation from dawn until dusk, year-round. The best way to visit the park is by public ferry from Boston or Hingham. Ferries operate from mid-May through mid-October.

Visitors may also access the islands by private vessel. Park moorings are open by reservation. Boaters are advised that there are no services such as visitor centers, restrooms, or drinking water outside of ferry operating hours.

Limited Access Islands

The Boston Harbor Islands are home to various wildlife. Many islands have limited access during bird breeding season from April through August, including Rainsford and Snake Islands, islands in Hingham Harbor (Sarah, Langlee, Ragged, and Button Island) and the outer islands (the Brewsters, Calf, Little Calf, and Green Island).

Private boater access to Thompson Island is limited to the beach area.

Prohibited Access Islands

Long, Gallops, Moon, Little Brewster, and The Graves are closed to public access.

Access By Private Boat

Visitors may travel to the park via private watercraft. Passenger drop-off at island floats is allowed with anchorage nearby. Always yield to park ferries. Note that not all islands have floats or safe places to anchor. Visit the boating page for more information.

Welcome Center

The Boston Harbor Islands National and State Park Welcome Center will be open for special events and programming throughout the summer. Check out our calendar for more details.

The Welcome Center is located on the Rose Kennedy Greenway at:

191W Atlantic Ave
Boston, MA 02110

Park Hours

The park is open from sunrise to sunset. If you arrived by public ferry, be mindful of when the last ferry departs the island.

Ferry Tickets

Reserve Your Seat for Your Next Island Visit!

Ferry service to the park is provided by Boston Harbor City Cruises . Scroll down to view and purchase ferry tickets for Georges and Spectacle Island online. Tickets can also be reserved over the phone at 617-227-4321, or in person at the ticket center at Long Wharf North. All ticket purchases are round-trip – there is no need to select a return time.

Visit the Peddocks Island ticketing page to reserve ferry travel to Peddocks Island.

Hingham to Georges Island ferry ticket purchases can be made on board the MBTA ferries that service Georges Island or at the ticket window at the MBTA Hingham Intermodal facility. Service from Hingham is offered by MBTA and is first-come, first-served. Note that Hingham to Georges Island ferry tickets cannot be purchased online and tickets purchased through the Boston Harbor City Cruises website or below are not valid for service from Hingham to Georges Island.
Visit the ferry schedule page for details on ferry routes, ticket pricing and terminals.
