Directions

The Aquarium and the Simons Theatre are located on Central Wharf in downtown Boston.

Directions by Car

The Aquarium and the Simons Theatre are located at 1 Central Wharf in Boston. We recommend reviewing and saving directions from your starting point in advance.

Directions by Foot

The Aquarium is just a short walk from the Faneuil Hall and Quincy Market area, the North End, Government Center, and the Financial District. View a map of the area around the Aquarium.

Directions by Public Transportation

The closest subway stop is the Aquarium stop on the MBTA’s Blue Line , just 100 yards from the Aquarium. All other subway lines connect to the Blue Line, and stops off all the other lines are only a short walk from the Aquarium:

Orange Line State Street stop: 8-minute walk

Green Line Government Center stop: 10-minute walk

Red Line South Station stop: 12-minute walk

Boat Services

Three commuter boat services land directly next to the Aquarium on Central Wharf. For complete schedules and ticket prices, visit each service’s website:

MBTA commuter boats depart from Hull, Hingham, and Logan Airport

Salem Ferry runs five times each day from Salem

The Winthrop Ferry , Valkyrie, departs Winthrop with stops at the Aquarium, Seaport, and Quincy

Parking

Though the Aquarium doesn’t own any parking facilities, there are five parking garages within .3 miles of the Aquarium. Parking validation at participating garages (75 State Street, Rowes Wharf, and Zero Post Office Square seven days a week—and One International Place weekdays after 4:30 p.m. and on weekends) is available for visitors at the Information Desk on Level 1 of the Aquarium. Note: Validation for the nearby Harbor Garage is available for Aquarium members and Whale Watch guests only.

Accessible Parking

Use this Aquarium accessible parking information sheet to find additional information like handicap-accessible parking spaces near MBTA stations and parking garage clearance for large vehicles.

Bus Parking

Many visitors, including school groups, come to the Aquarium via bus. Here, find parking information and directions for buses.

Bus Drop-off and Pickup

Please use the designated turnaround for drop-off and pickup. There is a 10-minute window for dropping off or picking up visitors, so please coordinate with your bus driver.

Parking Recommendation for Buses

300 Terminal Street (2.3 miles from the Aquarium). Please call 617-293-6247 for current parking fees.

Directions:

Travel to State Street to the first traffic light.

Turn right onto Atlantic Avenue.

Proceed north on Atlantic Avenue to a set of lights at the intersection with Causeway Street and North Washington Street, approximately one mile.

At the intersection, turn right onto North Washington Street (you will drive over a small expansion bridge).

At the first set of lights (City Square), turn right onto Chelsea Street.

Follow Chelsea Street to the end, which positions you under the Tobin Bridge.

Once under the bridge, you will travel over a small water channel.

Once over the channel, you will see signs on a wood fence for Bus Parking.

Turn right into the Mystic Pier 1 property and proceed to Station 1. Wait for the attendant to sign in.

Offers and Discounts

Offers and Discounts

The New England Aquarium offers a number of discounted admission options. Explore the list below to see if you qualify. For questions, call Reservations at 617-973-5206.

Free and Discounted Admission

New England Residents and New England College Students

Residents of CT, MA, ME, NH, RI, and VT receive $5 off standard Aquarium admission or admission + movie combinations. Students who attend college in New England (CT, MA, ME, NH, RI, and VT) may show their college ID to receive the New England Resident discount of $5 off standard Aquarium admission or admission + movie combinations. This offer cannot be combined with other offers or coupons. Blackout dates apply. The New England Resident discount is not available during weekends and on holidays in summer (Memorial Day weekend, May 23, 2026, to Labor Day weekend, September 7, 2026).

Boston CityPASS®

With one easy purchase, save up to 45% off admission to New England Aquarium, plus 3 more top Boston attractions: Museum of Science, Boston Harbor City Cruises, View Boston Observation Deck, Franklin Park Zoo, Harvard Museum of Natural History, and Museum of Fine Arts. Visit the attractions at your own pace, in any order, over 9-days for just $84 per adult and $72 per child (3-11). Learn more about CityPASS.

How To Redeem:

Buy CityPASS ® online .

Reserve your visit date and time through the My CityPASS® app or present your CityPASS tickets at the Box Office to receive the next available time slot.

Boston Family Days

Every student living in Boston enrolled in grades K-12 or Boston Pre-K will get free admission along with up to two guests to the New England Aquarium on the first and second Sundays of each month through the Boston Family Days program. Available to Boston school-aged children only and valid for Aquarium admission only on the first and second Sundays of the month until December 2026.

How To Redeem:

Onsite : Visit the Aquarium’s Box Office for entry that day. Please be prepared to show the Boston Family Days pass you received from BPS or the City of Boston before each program day. Please note: Entry is not guaranteed if the building is at capacity. Passes from BPS or the City of Boston must be registered in advance. We recommend registering for a pass for each student in your family.

Learn more about Boston Family Days .

Boston Public Library Coupon

All 26 branches of the Boston Public Library system offer individuals who have an active physical Boston Public Library card a coupon that allows free admission to the New England Aquarium for up to four (4) people on a specific day. This coupon may be redeemed at the Aquarium’s ticket booth. Valid only on the day printed on the coupon for Aquarium admission. This offer cannot be combined with other offers or coupons and cannot be applied to admission + movie combination tickets.

Please note: We are unable to accept screenshots of library coupons for admission.

Library Coupon from Participating Libraries

Many Massachusetts, New Hampshire, and Rhode Island public libraries have Aquarium coupons available for their library patrons. Coupons to the Aquarium are only valid on the date printed on the coupon. Each coupon admits up to four people at a discounted Aquarium admission price of 50% off per person. The discount applies to adult and child standard pricing Aquarium admission. Children two and under are always free. This offer cannot be combined with other offers or coupons and is not valid for movie or whale watch tickets.

Call your local library to see if it participates in the program, has available coupons, and for more information.

How To Redeem:

Contact your local library to reserve a coupon for New England Aquarium tickets.

Present your library coupons onsite at the Aquarium ticket booth to receive your ticket. Reservations not required. Visitors with library coupons are granted immediate entry after purchasing tickets even if the Aquarium is sold out to the general public at that time.

Please note: Library coupons must be reserved in advance through a participating library; there is no discount available by providing a library card. We are unable to accept screenshots of library coupons for admission.

Massachusetts EBT/WIC Card Holders

Show your Massachusetts EBT or WIC card and receive up to four admission tickets at a discounted rate of $5 per person. Available to Massachusetts residents only and valid any day the Aquarium is open. Not valid with any other offers or discounts. Not valid for whale watches or at the Simons Theatre. The Aquarium does not process EBT transactions—cash or other payment must be provided.

How To Redeem:

Onsite: Go to the Box Office with your EBT/WIC card to receive your discount and to reserve a timeslot. Please note: Due to limited capacity, some days, such as weekends, sell out in advance, and therefore entry is not guaranteed.

Online: Make a reservation here for your visit and provide payment (credit card only). You will receive an order confirmation via email. Bring your confirmation email on the date of your visit along with your EBT/WIC card to the Aquarium’s Box Office to receive your tickets for entry. Please note: Check your selected date and time carefully, as all ticket sales are final and will be honored only on the date and time noted.

This is available through the Card to Culture program. Learn more about the Card to Culture program . Please note: We do not accept ConnectorCare cards , Pandemic-EBT cards, or SUN Bucks (Summer EBT) cards for the EBT/WIC discount.

Massachusetts Teacher Association and Boston Teachers Union

Massachusetts Teacher Association or Boston Teachers Union card holders receive free Aquarium admission for themselves if they show their MTA or BTU card for the current academic year at the ticket booth.

This offer cannot be combined with other offers or coupons and is not valid for movie or whale watch tickets.

Veterans and Military

Active-duty U.S. military personnel and their dependents and U.S. military veterans receive a discount off standard pricing Aquarium admission. For active-duty military personnel, adults receive $10 off standard pricing Aquarium admission and children receive $5 off standard pricing Aquarium admission. This discount does not apply to nondependent guests accompanying military personnel. Both active-duty military personnel and their dependents need to show their military ID or military dependent card (orange/red) to receive the discount for themselves and their children. If the military personnel are not present, the spouse may show his or her military dependent card (orange/red) and a photo ID to receive the discount for themselves and their children. Veterans receive $10 off standard pricing for adult or senior Aquarium admission tickets by presenting proof of veteran status such as a DD forms, an NGB form, or a veteran’s ID card. This offer cannot be combined with other offers or coupons and is not valid for movie or whale watch tickets.

New England Nonprofit Organizations Reciprocity

The Aquarium is pleased to offer complimentary admission to employees of the participating organizations of the New England Nonprofit Arts/Cultural/Environmental Organizations reciprocity offering. The offer is valid for participating consortium employees only, you may purchase additional online tickets for guests. An employee ID is required at the scan station.

This offer cannot be combined with other offers or coupons and is not valid for movie or whale watch tickets. Please contact your human resources department to determine if your nonprofit organization is a participant of the reciprocity offering.

Accessibility

Guests using wheelchairs and visitors with visual impairments are welcome at the Aquarium free of charge. Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) defined service dogs are also welcome, but must be always under control due to the nature of the facility and the animals under our care.

membership benefits

Join the Aquarium Community

Visit the Aquarium

Cownose rays swim with loggerhead turtle Carolina in the Giant Ocean Tank
Photo: Vanessa Kahn

Each visit to the Aquarium supports our mission to inspire deeper connections to the ocean and actions to protect it.

Spring Hours : Open weekdays from 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m. and weekends from 9:00 a.m.–6:00 p.m.

Aquarium Tickets

Book your tickets in advance to ensure entry since weekends and holidays often sell out. Choose tickets for Aquarium admission, a film at Simons Theatre, a whale watch cruise, or a combination.

BUY TICKETS

A sea lion lifts its flipper

Photo: Caitlin Cunningham

Simons Theatre

The Simons Theatre screens a collection of 3D nature documentaries and films on the biggest movie screen in New England each day.

SEE WHAT'S PLAYING

The Simons Theatre

Photo: Allegro Photography

Daily Presentations and Feeding Schedule

Visitors are invited to enjoy daily presentations in the Marine Mammal Center, at the top of the Giant Ocean Tank, or at the Harbor Seal Exhibit. Watch our penguin aquarists feed the penguins daily and ask questions about our animals and their habitat. This schedule is subject to change. Please confirm times at the Information Desk upon arrival.

New England Aquarium Whale Watch

The New England Aquarium partners with Boston Harbor City Cruises to give visitors front-row seats to view some of the ocean’s most fascinating animals. All cruises leave right from Central Wharf. Join us, and save 10% off Aquarium tickets when you buy tickets to the Whale Watch Cruise! Call us at 617-973-5206 or visit our box office to apply your discount.
