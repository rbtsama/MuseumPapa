Visit NBWM

Ignite your curiosity and discover the vibrant stories of our region through an interactive journey into art, history, and science. From massive whale skeletons to hands-on maritime adventures, it’s a family-friendly experience that truly brings the spirit of exploration to life.

 

Learn More

Visit NBWM

At the New Bedford Whaling Museum, we like things big—step aboard the Lagoda, the world’s largest half-scale model of a whaling bark. Then walk beneath full whale skeletons of four species—right, blue, humpback, and sperm—to experience their immense scale and compare their unique forms.

 

Learn More

Proteanna: Susan Heideman

Artist Susan Heideman explores organic, ocean-inspired forms that evoke both real and imagined invertebrate life, suggesting primordial creatures drifting in deep waters. Her Proteanna series features vibrant, large-scale, hand-embroidered works layered with paintings and constructions, creating richly textured, almost three-dimensional environments that immerse viewers in an imaginative underwater world.

 

On View: May 29 - November 1, 2026

New 3D film!

Wonders of the Arctic

Set in the frozen wilderness at the top of the world, Wonders of the Arctic tells the story of survival in one of the most unforgiving environments on earth dominated by a single element: ice. Faraway in this glittering world of ice and snow, polar bears tussle, huskies howl and narwhals dive within the turquoise waters. Through compelling stories told by scientists and Inuit leaders, viewers will discover the impact of ice on the Arctic’s inhabitants, both animals and humans.
