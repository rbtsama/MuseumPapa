Accessibility

We strive to create engaging, accessible experiences for all. Boston By Foot offers a wide range of tours, virtual programs and volunteer opportunities. There are accessible ways to enjoy many of our activities. We look forward to exploring Boston with you!

It helps to understand Boston’s history and geography. Boston is a city of made land. Boston was once 800-acres of steep hills, meadows and swamps. Over time, land was created, most of our cobblestone streets replaced, and some hills leveled. The city today still has many narrow paths, some brick and cobblestone sidewalks, and a few hills. We also have neighborhoods of flat made land, and wide paths.

Plan Your Tour

We understand that everyone experiences their disability differently. We offer a range of services to help you have a great tour.

Physical or Mobility Needs

  • In each tour description we provide a terrain rating. If you have limited mobility, our tours with a Terrain 3 rating (likely uneven surfaces and significant inclines) are the least accessible. Fortunately, we have lots of other choices. You can search for tours by terrain rating by clicking on the terrain rating icon.

    Terrain 1: Mostly flat surfaces and little to no inclines

    Terrain 2: Potential uneven surfaces and moderate inclines

    Terrain 3: Likely uneven surfaces and significant inclines

  • Most tours start and end in different places. Each tour description lists the tour meeting and end location, and if the nearest MBTA transit station is accessible.

  • Boston By Foot welcomes your personal walkers, wheelchairs, scooters and other mobility devices. We are unable to provide assistance with pushing manual wheelchairs or other mobility devices while on tours.

  • We are happy to provide you with information in advance on local wheelchair rental companies. Call 617-367-2345 or email us at info@bostonbyfoot.org

  • Personal care attendants assisting a guest with a disability are admitted free of charge. Please contact us at 617-367-2345 or email us at info@bostonbyfoot.org to reserve a free ticket.

Deaf or Hearing Loss

  • Assisted Listening devices or sign-language interpreters may be requested for any tour. Please provide us with two-week notice to make arrangements. Call 617-367-2345 or email us at info@bostonbyfoot.org

  • Virtual programs have captions.

  • Most tours start and end in different places. Each tour description lists the tour meeting and end location, and if the nearest MBTA transit station is accessible.

Blind or Low Vision

  • Many tours incorporate tactile elements and our tour guides are trained to use descriptive language.

  • Most tours start and end in different places. Each tour description lists the tour meeting and end location, and if the nearest MBTA transit station is accessible.
  • We are unable to provide sighted guide assistance. Sighted guides or personal care attendants assisting a guest with a disability are admitted free of charge. Please contact us at 617-367-2345 or email us at info@bostonbyfoot.org to reserve a free ticket.

Service Animals

Boston By Foot welcomes service animals and their handlers at all tours and programs. We follow the Americans with Disabilities Act definition of a service animal. This is a dog that is individually trained to do work or perform specific tasks for an individual with a disability. A service animal may also be a miniature horse. (Pets are welcome only on private tours with advance notice.)

We train our staff and volunteers to welcome people using service animals and to respect your medical privacy. Unless there is a concern regarding your animal’s behavior, we will assume it is a service animal and not inquire. If you have experienced unwanted attention, please let us know at 617-367-2345 or info@bostonbyfoot.org

How are We Doing? - We Value Your Feedback

Boston By Foot is committed to removing barriers and creating accessible and inclusive experiences, and we value feedback and input from people with disabilities. If you identify as a person with a disability, we invite you to join us for a free tour and to share your experiences with accessibility in a brief after tour Accessibility Survey. Please contact us at info@bostonbyfoot.org or 617-367-2345 to reserve your free tour ticket and provide your feedback on our Accessibility Survey.

Contact Us

If you did not find answers to your accessibility questions, please reach out to us at 617-367-2345 or info@bostonbyfoot.org. We can provide tour-planning advice tailored to your individual needs. We also welcome your suggestions for removing barriers and creating more enjoyable experiences. If you feel you have been denied meaningful access to Boston By Foot’s services, programs and/or activities on the basis of your disability, or if you have had a negative experience with Boston By Foot, please contact us at 617-367-2345 or info@bostonbyfoot.org
