Rich History
The Public Health Museum is a non-profit educational and cultural museum. The Museum strives to preserve records and artifacts from our nation’s public health history; educate the public about the achievements and contributions of public health, and inspire people to build upon the past and continue to advance the future of public health. 
Sign up for tours below!
Docent guided museum tours are by reservation only. 
Walk-ins are encouraged, but docent guided tours cannot be ensured. 

If you must cancel, please let us know as soon as possible so that we may give the spot to someone else.
Refunds will not be given to those unable to attend their booked tours.
The museum is all volunteer staffed and relies on tours to fund its work.

Free Tour Bookings
The Public Health Museum provides free tours to:
  • Children
  • Veterans and Active Military
  • Museum Members
  • Organizational Sponsors
  • Tewksbury Hospital Employees and Patients
  • Library Pass Holders
  • Admit One Ticket Holders
How to book your free tour:

For Library Pass Holders:
Currently the Public Health Museum has library passes with these local libraries: Haverhill Public Library, Nevins Memorial Library, Tewksbury Public Library, and Wilmington Memorial Library.
To book either an indoor or outdoor tour, please use the Library Pass ticket option. At checkout you will be prompted to enter a promo code (which can be found on your printed library pass). This code must be used at checkout to get a free tour.
Please provide your printed pass to museum staff at your arrival. 

For Tewksbury Hospital Employees and Patients:
The Public Health Museum has shared a promo code with Tewksbury Hospital administration. 
To book either an indoor or outdoor tour, please use the Tewksbury Hospital Employee/Patient ticket option. The provided promo code must be used at checkout to get a free tour.

For Admit One Pass Holders:
All Public Health Museum Admit One tickets will have a specific promo code written on the back of the card. 
To book either an indoor or outdoor tour, please use the PHM Admit One ticket option. At checkout you will be prompted to enter a promo code (which can be found on the back of the card). This code must be used at checkout to get a free tour.
Please provide your Admit One ticket to museum staff at your arrival.
*For those who have received a Admit One Pass before March 2026: please reach out to the museum to book your free tour by emailing us at [email protected]

Paranormal Groups and Organizations

As we are situated on a historic campus with an operating hospital on the grounds, the Museum is committed to respecting the privacy of patients past and present. 
​
The Museum aims to provide educational experiences based on scientific research and evidence. Providing additional services to organizations, groups, and/or companies that are predicated in the paranormal does not fall within the mission or scope of the Public Health Museum.
We are proud members of the Massachusetts History Alliance , Digital Commonwealth, Massachusetts Office of Travel and Tourism, Mass Cultural Council, American Association for State and Local History, and the Librarians, Archivists, and Museum Professionals in the History of the Health Sciences.

Driving Directions
GPS Address:

Mailing Address:
​365 East Street
Tewksbury, MA 01876
From Route 93:
Take the Dascomb Road exit west toward Tewksbury.  Dascomb road turns into East Street.  Just past the intersection of Livingston Street and East street, you will see the hospital grounds.  Take the first driveway on your left, past the large no smoking sign.  Park in the first parking lot you see as you enter.  The Museum is housed in the Old Administration Building, with the tall green spire, which is just behind the Chapel.
From Route 495:
Take Exit 38 south to Tewksbury.  On Route 38 South, follow signs to the hospital and museum just beyond the police station at the Town Green.  Turn left at the set of lights in the town center onto East Street.  Approximately one mile down East Street you will see the Old Administration Building on your right.  Pass this building and take your next right on to the hospital grounds.  Park in the first parking lot you see as you enter.  The Museum is housed in the Old Administration Building, with the tall green spire, which is just behind the Chapel.
