Hours and Admission

Hours

The museum is open from 1:00-4:00 pm Saturday and Sunday from June 6 – November 15.

Tours of the mansion are guided and begin at the top of the hour.  Admission to the Slave Quarters, which also houses the archaeological exhibit, gift shop, and restrooms, is free of charge.

Group Tours

During our tour season, no reservation is required for groups of fewer than 10 people. Groups of 10 or more are considered group tours and must have a reservation. Visit our Group Tours page for group rates and scheduling. Visit our School Groups page for information regarding school group tours.

Admission

Admission is always FREE for Royall House and Slave Quarters members. Learn how to join.

We offer free admission to active duty military personnel and their families. Blue Star Museums is a program that offers free admission to museums for all active duty, National Guard, and Reserve military personnel and their families from Memorial Day through Labor Day.

We offer free admission to current or retired members of the Massachusetts Teachers Association upon presentation of a valid MTA card.

We offer discounted admission to EBT and WIC cardholders through the Mass Cultural Council’s Card to Culture program, offered in collaboration with the Department of Transitional Assistance, the WIC Nutrition Program, and the Massachusetts Health Connector. See the complete lists of participating organizations offering EBT, WIC, and ConnectorCare discounts.

Photography

Noncommercial still photography is permitted inside the Royall mansion and the Slave Quarters as well as on the grounds as long as it does not interfere with tours or other programs. “Selfie sticks,” tripods, and other similar accessories are not permitted indoors. Filming/videotaping is not allowed without specific prior permission.

News and Events

We have many exciting events planned throughout the year. Learn more here!

Using Material Culture to Interpret Enslavement
Wednesday, May 13, 2026, Noon – 1 PM, Online
Join the Interpreting Enslavement and Freedom Community of Practice to consider the challenges and opportunities around interpreting enslavement with material culture. Staff from the Royall House and Slave Quarters (Medford, MA) will share the ways they are using archaeological finds, as well as household furnishings, to explore the lives of enslaved folks and provide richer, nuanced interpretation of their experiences. Bring your thoughts and questions to discuss with peers!
Register here

Durag and Headscarf Workshop –
May 2 and 3 or May 23 and 24 , 1:00-4:00 PM
Come learn how to make a dye-dyed, hand-sewn durag or headscarf using natural dyes. The workshops are free and open to BIPOC participants.  There are limited slots for each two-day workshop.  If you are interested, email director@royallhouse.org.
